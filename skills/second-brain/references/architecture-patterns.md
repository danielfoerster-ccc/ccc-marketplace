# Architecture Patterns by User Context

*Reference file for second-brain skill — loaded during Phase 2 (Architect) when customizing sub-folder suggestions.*

## Entrepreneur / Business Owner

Focus: Operations-heavy, multiple active ventures, client work, AI tooling

```
00 - COMMAND CENTER
    ├── Vision & Identity
    ├── 90-Day Sprint (active)
    ├── Weekly Plans
    └── Daily Notes/YYYY/MM/

01 - KNOWLEDGE BASE
    ├── Mental Models
    ├── Thinkers & Philosophers
    ├── Business & Strategy Concepts
    ├── Health & Performance
    └── Culture (books, films, etc.)

02 - MISSION CONTROL
    ├── Claude Skills & Plugins
    ├── SOPs & Playbooks
    ├── Prompt Templates
    └── Workflows & Systems

03 - OPERATIONS
    ├── [Venture 1]
    ├── [Venture 2]
    ├── Finance
    └── Relationships & Network

04 - ARCHIVE
```

**Emphasis:** Command Center and Mission Control are daily-use folders. Operations contains one subfolder per active business. Finance and personal areas live inside Operations too.

## Knowledge Worker / Executive

Focus: Projects, meeting notes, decision logs, industry knowledge

```
00 - COMMAND CENTER
    ├── Goals & Priorities
    ├── Weekly Plans
    └── Daily Notes/

01 - KNOWLEDGE BASE
    ├── Mental Models
    ├── Industry & Domain Knowledge
    ├── People & Leaders
    └── Book Notes

02 - MISSION CONTROL
    ├── Workflows & Processes
    ├── Meeting Templates
    └── AI Prompts

03 - OPERATIONS
    ├── Active Projects/
    ├── Ongoing Responsibilities/
    └── Finance & Admin

04 - ARCHIVE
```

**Emphasis:** Knowledge Base is core — industry expertise is the competitive advantage. Mission Control is lighter (fewer AI tools) but important for repeatable workflows.

## Student / Researcher

Focus: Course notes, research, reading, learning systems

```
00 - COMMAND CENTER
    ├── Semester Goals
    ├── Weekly Study Plan
    └── Daily Notes/

01 - KNOWLEDGE BASE
    ├── Core Concepts (by discipline)
    ├── Mental Models
    ├── Thinkers & Researchers
    └── Reading Notes

02 - MISSION CONTROL
    ├── Study Methods & Templates
    ├── Research Workflows
    └── Writing Templates

03 - OPERATIONS
    ├── Active Courses/
    ├── Research Projects/
    └── Administrative

04 - ARCHIVE
```

**Emphasis:** Knowledge Base is the primary asset — this person is building a lifelong knowledge library. Operations is mostly course folders. Mission Control is lighter (study templates, writing frameworks).

## Creative (Writer, Designer, Content Creator)

Focus: Ideas, projects, inspiration, output tracking

```
00 - COMMAND CENTER
    ├── Creative Vision
    ├── Active Projects Status
    └── Daily Notes/

01 - KNOWLEDGE BASE
    ├── Ideas & Concepts
    ├── Influences & Inspiration
    ├── Craft & Technique
    └── Culture (the good stuff)

02 - MISSION CONTROL
    ├── Creative Workflows
    ├── AI Prompts & Tools
    └── Publishing SOPs

03 - OPERATIONS
    ├── Active Projects/
    ├── Client Work/ (if applicable)
    └── Business & Finance

04 - ARCHIVE
```

**Emphasis:** Knowledge Base has an "Ideas & Concepts" folder that's treated like a creative garden — something to browse, not just file things into. Mission Control includes publishing and content workflows.

## Notes on Sub-Folder Depth

Regardless of context, keep sub-folder depth to **2 levels maximum inside each top-level folder.** Deeper nesting creates navigation friction and makes it harder for AI tools to reliably locate content.

Good: `03 - OPERATIONS / Venture Name / Client Name.md`
Avoid: `03 - OPERATIONS / Venture Name / Clients / Active / Client Name / Notes / 2026 /`

The PKM tool's search and linking features exist precisely to compensate for flat structure — you don't need folders to find things, you need them to *organize* things.
