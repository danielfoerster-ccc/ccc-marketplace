## TAPs Full Reference — By Revenue Tier

### TIER A: €0–250K (Pre-seed, early-stage freelancer/agency)

| Allocation | Target % | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Profit | 5% | Minimal reinvestment; focus is on founder income |
| Owner's Comp | 50% | Founder priority; business exists to pay founder |
| Tax | 15% | Standard EU rate (self-employed + VAT) |
| Operating Expenses | 30% | Lean operation; mostly founder working alone |
| **TOTAL** | **100%** | |

**CCC Context:** Not primary target. Pre-revenue or very early freelance.

**When to use this tier:** Founder has < €250K real revenue. Usually solo operator or pre-team.

| Allocation | Target % | Rationale |
|---|---|---|

## TAPs Adjustment Rules

TAPs are benchmarks, not law. Adjust when context demands it.

### 1. Industry Norms

**When to adjust:** Your business model has fundamentally different cost structure than average.

**Examples:**
- **High-touch consulting:** May run 50–60% OpEx (more than standard 40–50%) due to higher staff intensity
- **SaaS with infrastructure costs:** May run 60%+ OpEx (servers, R&D, support) temporarily while scaling
- **E-commerce/fulfillment:** May run 70%+ OpEx during ramp (inventory, logistics)
- **Legal/accounting services:** May run lower OpEx (mainly salary) — adjust Profit higher
- **Marketplace/platform:** Variable OpEx until network effects kick in

**How to adjust:** Increase OpEx by 5–10 points; decrease Profit (and/or Owner's Comp if very high) correspondingly. Document the reason.

### 2. Business Stage (Growth vs. Stable)

**When to adjust:** Business is explicitly growth-phase or explicitly mature.

**Examples:**
- **Growth phase (reinvesting heavily):** Increase OpEx by 5%, decrease Profit by 5% (short-term trade)
- **Mature/stable (cash harvest):** Decrease OpEx by 5%, increase Profit by 5% (shift to harvest)
- **Post-pivot recovery:** Increase OpEx by 10% temporarily (infrastructure rebuild), decrease Profit

**How to adjust:** Shift 5–10 points between Profit and OpEx, or reduce Owner's Comp if needed. Make it explicit this is temporary (e.g., "growth phase, revisit in 18 months").

### 3. Owner's Personal Situation

**When to adjust:** Founder's financial needs or constraints differ from average.

**Examples:**
- **Founder has outside income:** Can reduce Owner's Comp target 5–10% (not dependent on this business)
- **High personal expenses / dependents:** Increase Owner's Comp target 5–10% (need higher salary)
- **Founder is bootstrapped, no investors:** May prioritize Profit higher (cash buffer for uncertainty)
- **Founder has significant personal debt:** May increase Owner's Comp short-term to pay down, then shift to Profit

**How to adjust:** Shift 5–10 points between Owner's Comp and Profit. Document the personal constraint. Plan to revisit in 12 months.

### 4. Tax Jurisdiction Variation

**When to adjust:** Actual tax rate in jurisdiction differs from 15% EU standard.

**Examples:**
- **US self-employed:** Typically 25–30% (FICA + income tax + state)
- **UK sole proprietor:** 20% (income tax) + 9% (NI) = ~28–30%
- **Germany GmbH with profit:** 15% (corporate) + 26% (income tax if distributed) = 40% effective
- **Singapore / low-tax jurisdiction:** 5–10% corporate, lower personal

**How to adjust:** Update Tax allocation to actual accountant estimate. Adjust Profit downward if tax moves up (zero-sum).

## Real Revenue Calculation Formula

### Definition

**Real Revenue** = Total Revenue - Pass-Through Costs

Where "pass-through costs" are revenues billed to clients that don't belong to the business (COGS, subcontractor labor, vendor costs, etc.).

Real Revenue is the only valid denominator for calculating allocations. Using gross revenue inflates every metric and masks true cash health.

### Pass-Through Costs

**Included (subtract from revenue):**
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) if product business
- Raw materials, components, manufacturing
- Subcontractor labor (freelancers, agencies hired for client work)
- Vendor costs billed directly to clients (tools, software, media spend)
- Commission or referral fees billed to clients
- Third-party licensing or IP billed to clients

**NOT included (don't subtract):**
- Employee payroll (this is OpEx)
- Office rent, utilities, infrastructure (your costs)
- Internal tools, software subscriptions (your infrastructure)
- Owner salary or draws (this is Owner's Comp allocation)
- Taxes (this is Tax allocation)
- Marketing for your own business (this is OpEx)

### By Business Type

**Product Business (SaaS, physical product, hybrid):**
```
Real Revenue = Total Revenue - COGS
(COGS = materials + manufacturing + fulfillment + hosting + payment processing)
```

Example: SaaS with €1M revenue, €150K server/processing costs.
```
Real Revenue = €1M - €150K = €850K
```

**Agency / Service Business (digital, marketing, consulting):**
```
Real Revenue = Total Revenue - (Subcontractors + Media Spend Passed + Tools Billed)
```

Example: Digital agency with €500K revenue, €150K freelancers, €80K media spend, €20K tools.
```
Real Revenue = €500K - (€150K + €80K + €20K) = €250K
```

**Pure Service (coaching, consulting, legal, accounting):**
```
Real Revenue ≈ Total Revenue (usually minimal pass-through)

Exception: If you license IP or research and bill separately, subtract that cost.
```

Example: Management consulting with €300K revenue, €0 pass-through.
```
Real Revenue = €300K - €0 = €300K
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Real Revenue is the denominator.** No Real Revenue, no valid diagnosis.
2. **CAPs reveal truth.** Allocations don't change until behavior changes.
3. **TAPs are anchors, not commandments.** Adjust for context, but lock them early.
4. **The Bleed is precise.** It quantifies exactly what needs to shift.
5. **Rollout plans are quarterly roadmaps.** 1–3% per quarter, not overnight jumps.
6. **Founder agreement is the gate.** If founder doesn't understand The Bleed, stop and revisit.
7. **Quarterly verification is ongoing.** Assessment is diagnostic; execution requires tracking.
