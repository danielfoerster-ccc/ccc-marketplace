## Workflow: Six-Phase Quarterly Checkpoint

The quarterly review is the **behavioral heartbeat** of Profit First. It runs every 90 days and keeps the system alive through structured progress measurement, tangible profit distribution rewards, and deliberate adjustment. This skill guides you through all six phases in one session.

**Outcome:** Updated CAPs, profit distribution executed, next quarter's allocation percentages set, and forward plan documented.

### The 4Rs Framework (Profit First Professionals Standard)

The six phases align with the official 4R quarterly review structure:

- **Revisit** (Phases 1-2) — "Let's look at where you were." Gather historical data, review previous goals and allocation percentages (CAPs), establish baseline.
- **Review** (Phase 2-3) — "Let's look at the changes you've made." Assess current performance, calculate new CAPs, compare to targets (TAPs), analyze The Bleed, celebrate profit distribution.
- **Reap** (Phase 3) — "Let's celebrate!" Execute the 50/50 profit distribution. Founder receives tangible reward for discipline.
- **Reach** (Phases 4-5) — "Let's discuss the next step." Set new allocation percentages for next quarter, select 2-3 Profit Points focus areas, identify upcoming cash flow events, plan forward strategy.

## Prerequisites

You must have already:
- Completed the [[ccc-cashflow-instant-assessment]] (initial system setup)
- Completed at least one quarterly review (or this is Q1, your first review)
- Have access to: bank statements for all 5+ accounts, P&L statement, previous quarter's review (for comparison)

### Special Case: First-Ever Quarterly Review (Q1)

If this is the founder's first review with no previous quarter:

- **Baseline:** Use Instant Assessment CAPs as your starting point.
- **Conservative targets:** 1-2% adjustments only. Prove execution before heroic changes.
- **Skip trends:** You have no historical data yet. Q1 establishes baseline; Q2 shows real progress.
- **Measure execution:** Did you follow allocations? Execute distribution? Identify what worked? These matter more than comparisons.
- **Document:** "This is Q1, baseline quarter. Opening CAPs from Instant Assessment. Next quarter we'll compare QoQ progress."
- **Plan forward:** Select 2-3 Profit Points with measurable targets. Forward planning is your leverage in Q1.

## Mode Detection: Consultant vs. Self-Service

**Consultant Mode:** You're running the review WITH a client. Ask questions, guide each phase, produce a polished client report at the end.

**Self-Service Mode:** Business owner reviewing their own numbers. Lean, direct, self-driven. Minimal explanation.

At the start, ask: "Are you running this review with a client (consultant mode) or reviewing your own business (self-service mode)?"

## Phase 1: Preparation (Gather Financial Data)

**Duration:** 60–90 minutes | **Owner:** Accountant or Finance Lead

### What You Need
- [ ] Bank statements for all accounts (Owner's Comp, Profit, OpEx, Tax, Contingency, + any others)
- [ ] Final P&L statement for the quarter
- [ ] Previous quarter's Instant Assessment or quarterly review (for CAP comparison)
- [ ] Current allocation percentages from rollout plan
- [ ] List of major revenue or expense events this quarter

### Steps

1. **Gather financial data:** Bank statements (all accounts), P&L, previous CAPs/TAPs, current rollout targets. If using CDT, download quarterly data.

2. **Verify balances:** Owner's Comp $____, Profit $____, OpEx $____, Tax $____, Contingency $____, Other $____.

3. **Load baseline:** Previous CAPs and TAPs for quarter-over-quarter comparison.

4. **Note execution:** Record target percentages for this quarter vs. actual execution.

## Phase 2: Updated Assessment (Calculate CAPs)

**Duration:** 60–90 minutes | **Outcome:** Real Revenue, CAPs, Bleed calculation, progress narrative

### Deliverables
- [ ] Real Revenue calculated for the quarter
- [ ] Current Allocation Percentages (CAPs) for all accounts
- [ ] Cashflow Business Score (CBS) calculated
- [ ] Comparison matrix (CAPs vs. last quarter, CAPs vs. TAPs)
- [ ] Bleed calculation and trend
- [ ] Written progress narrative (1 paragraph)

### Steps

1. **Calculate Real Revenue for the quarter**
   ```
   Real Revenue = Total Invoiced - (Refunds + Chargebacks)
   ```
   This is the money that actually hit accounts. Conservative and accurate.

2. **Calculate CAPs:** CAP = Account Balance / (Real Revenue ÷ 3 months). Example: $28,500 balance / $40,000 monthly avg = 71.25% OpEx. Do this for all five accounts.

2b. **Calculate CBS (Cashflow Business Score):** CBS = Profit% + Owner's Comp% + Tax%. Track quarter-over-quarter for health trajectory.

3. **Compare CAPs to last quarter's CAPs**
   - Create comparison table: Account | Last Quarter | This Quarter | Change
   - Are allocations moving in the right direction?
   - Profit and Owner's Comp increasing? OpEx decreasing or stable?

4. **Compare CAPs to TAPs (Target Allocation Percentages)**
   Use this table to find your revenue band:
   
   | Revenue Range | Profit | Owner's Pay | Tax | OpEx |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | $0–250K | 5% | 50% | 15% | 30% |
   | $250K–500K | 10% | 35% | 15% | 40% |
   | $500K–1M | 15% | 20% | 15% | 50% |
   | $1M–5M | 10% | 10% | 15% | 65% |
   | $5M–10M | 15% | 5% | 15% | 65% |
   | $10M–50M | 20% | 0% | 15% | 65% |

5. **Calculate The Bleed:** Sum all gaps between CAPs and TAPs. Example: Owner's Comp -1%, Profit -1%, OpEx +12%, Tax -2%, Contingency -1% = 8% total Bleed. Track narrowing quarter-over-quarter to measure improvement.

6. **Write progress narrative:** One paragraph. Tone: factual, celebratory of wins, honest about gaps. Example: "Profit 3%→5% (+2%), real revenue +12%, OpEx flat, Owner's Comp +1%, Bleed narrowed 3%. Tracking to 12-month targets."

## Phase 3: Profit Distribution (The Behavioral Reward)

**Duration:** 15–30 minutes | **Critical rule:** Always distribute 50% of Profit account — even if small.

The profit distribution is non-negotiable. This is how the founder *feels* the system works.

### Steps

1. **Verify Profit balance:** $______.

2. **Calculate 50/50 split:** Owner Reward = Balance × 0.50 | Profit Savings = Balance × 0.50.

3. **Execute transfer:** Same-week to founder's personal account. Confirm: [ ] Yes | Date: ______.

4. **Celebrate:** Founder communicates to team: "This quarter's distribution is $X. That's proof the system works." Frame as evidence of discipline, not a bonus.

5. **Edge case (low balance):** Still distribute 50%, even if $250. Habit matters more than amount. Plan: Increase Profit allocation next quarter.

---

## Phase 4: Adjustment (Set Next Quarter's Percentages)

**Duration:** 30–45 minutes | **Principle:** Adjust 1–3% per quarter, never more. Compound discipline beats heroic moves.

### Steps

1. **Standard (±5% revenue):** Profit +1-2%, Owner's Comp +1-2%, OpEx -2-4%, Tax & Contingency stable.

2. **Growth (+10%+ revenue):** Keep percentages; let dollar amounts grow with revenue. This funds growth without breaking discipline.

3. **Contraction (-10%+ revenue):** Maintain percentages; accounts self-adjust to lower amounts. Creates pressure on OpEx to cut.

4. **Major expense planned:** Increase OpEx to cover cost, reduce Profit/Owner's Comp to offset. Document and communicate rationale.

5. **Document:** Create table: Account | Last Quarter CAP | This Quarter CAP | Next Quarter Target | Rationale.

## Phase 5: Forward Planning (12 Profit Points & Upcoming Events)

**Duration:** 45–60 minutes | **Output:** Next quarter's focus areas (2–3 points), specific targets, cash flow events, forward plan document

### The 12 Profit Points

Score each 1–5 at every review:

1. **Pricing** — Are rates optimal?
2. **Volume** — Are we acquiring enough customers?
3. **Efficiency** — Are processes lean?
4. **Waste Elimination** — Are we cutting non-essentials?
5. **Leverage** — Are we using systems/automation?
6. **Client Quality** — Are we serving profitable clients?
7. **Payment Terms** — Are we collecting fast enough?
8. **Contingency Reserve** — Is it at 3 months of OpEx?
9. **Cash Velocity** — How fast does money cycle?
10. **Expense Control** — Is OpEx discipline holding?
11. **Revenue Concentration** — Are we diversified?
12. **Debt Management** — Do we have unsecured obligations?

### Steps

1. **Review last quarter's scorecard:** Which points improved? Stalled? Declined?

2. **Assess focused points:** Did your 2-3 selected points improve? Document evidence (e.g., "Pricing: +8% rates, zero loss, score 4/5 was 2/5").

3. **Select 2-3 for next quarter:** Highest impact, team capacity, low-hanging fruit first.

4. **Set measurable targets:** "Reduce DSO 45→35 days" not "improve collection." Must be assessable at next review.

5. **Upcoming cash flow events:** Tax dates, annual renewals, seasonal dips/spikes, one-time expenses, client patterns.

6. **Adjust reserves:** Contingency < 3mo OpEx? Allocate next quarter. Profit Savings > 6mo? Room to increase allocations.

7. **Document forward plan** to `[Vault]/03 - OPERATIONS/Claude Cowork Consultants/[Client]/Quarterly Reviews/Q[N] Forward Plan.md`: Profit Points, cash flow events, reserve strategy, success metrics.

### Phase 5b: Seven Discussion Topics (Consultant-Led Reviews)

For consultant engagements, offer these focus areas: *"Which would you like to focus on next quarter?"*

1. **Margins** — Raise prices? Reduce delivery costs?
2. **Services** — Right clients? Sunset low-margin offerings?
3. **Systems** — Automation, delegation, process redesign?
4. **Investments** — Strategic software, tools, team hires?
5. **Growth** — Adjacent services, new markets, segments?
6. **Health** — Insurance, emergency reserves, suppliers?
7. **Expenses** — Non-essential OpEx to cut, renegotiate, consolidate?

Founder selects 1-2 to combine with 2-3 Profit Points focus. Ensures plan covers both financial mechanics and strategy.

## Phase 6: Documentation & Close Loop

**Duration:** 30–45 minutes | **Output:** Completed review document, next review scheduled

### Deliverables
- [ ] Quarterly Review document completed and dated
- [ ] CDT (Cashflow Development Tool) updated (if applicable)
- [ ] Client report prepared (if consultant engagement)
- [ ] Review notes saved to vault
- [ ] Next quarterly review scheduled (90 days out, on calendar)

### Steps

1. **Complete Quarterly Review document** using template. Sections: Executive Overview, Real Revenue & Assessment, CAPs, Bleed Analysis, Profit Distribution, Next Quarter Adjustments, Profit Points, Forward Plan, Sign-Off. Both sign/date. Save to `Q[N] 2026 — Quarterly Review.md`.

2. **Update CDT (if in use):** Real Revenue (annualized + monthly), CAPs ($€%), TAPs, Delta/Korrektur (EU adjustment tracking), CBS-Score, Rollout plan progression. No CDT? Create simple sheet: Date | Real Revenue | Owner's Comp | Profit | OpEx | Tax | Contingency | Bleed | CBS-Score.

3. **Consultant engagement:** Prepare 1-page client report (progress, metrics, distribution, priorities, recommendations). Share within 5 business days.

4. **Schedule next review:** 90 days out, 3-hour block, with founder/accountant/consultant. Send invite 3 weeks prior with prep checklist.

## Rules

1. **Always distribute 50% of Profit.** Small rewards > no rewards. Habit matters more than amount.

2. **Distribute within 3 business days.** Same-week execution. Delay kills psychological reward.

3. **Compare to previous quarter.** Always load last quarter's CAPs first. QoQ comparison only.

4. **Max 3% adjustment per quarter.** Compound 1-2% beats heroic moves. More sustainable and predictable.

5. **Never skip planning.** Numbers backward-looking; Profit Points focus forward-looking. Both essential.

6. **Select 2-3 Profit Points max.** 4+ causes loss of focus.

7. **Measurable targets only.** "Reduce DSO 45→35 days," not "collect faster."

8. **Schedule next review before closing.** Calendar entry non-negotiable. Attach prep checklist to invite.

9. **Update CDT during Phase 6.** CDT is the system's memory. Finalize before closing review.

## Key Success Metrics (How to Know the System Is Working)

| Metric | Target | What It Means |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| **The Bleed (total gap to TAPs)** | Narrowing by 2–6% per quarter | Business is moving toward targets. |
| **Profit distribution executed** | Every quarter, no exceptions | Founder is being rewarded. System is behavioral. |
| **Review completed on schedule** | Within 7 days of quarter end | Discipline is holding. Late reviews signal trouble. |
| **Next quarter's allocation percentages documented** | Before the new quarter starts | Planning is happening. Percentages are communicated. |
| **Profit Points focus selected** | 2–3 per quarter, tracked | Strategy is intentional. Focus drives improvement. |
| **Profit account balance increasing** | Quarter-over-quarter growth | The system is accumulating profit (ultimate signal). |
