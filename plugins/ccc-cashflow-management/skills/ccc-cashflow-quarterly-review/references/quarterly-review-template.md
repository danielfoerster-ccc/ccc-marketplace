## Current Allocation Percentages (CAPs)

**Calculation basis:** Real Revenue for quarter: $________ (3 months = $________/month average)

| **Other** | __% | __% | __% | — | [If applicable] |
| **TOTAL** | 100% | 100% | 100% | — | ✓ Balanced |

**How to calculate CAPs:**
```
For each account:
CAP = Current Balance / (Real Revenue ÷ 3 months)

Example: 
Real Revenue = $151,000 (Q2 2026)
Monthly average = $151,000 ÷ 3 = $50,333
OpEx Account balance = $31,250
OpEx CAP = $31,250 ÷ $50,333 = 62%
```

## Notes on Using These Templates

1. **Fill in numbers as you calculate them during the review.** Don't try to complete the entire document from memory afterward.

2. **The "Rationale" and "Interpretation" sections are where the insight lives.** Numbers alone don't tell the story. Why did OpEx increase? What explains the profit distribution size? Use these sections to create narrative context.

3. **Founder and accountant both sign.** This is a shared commitment. Signatures matter — they signal accountability and care.

4. **Save with clear naming:** `Q[N] 2026 — Quarterly Review & Analysis.md` so you can find it easily at next quarter's review for comparison.

5. **The template is a starting point.** If your business has unique accounts or special circumstances, adapt. The structure matters more than matching every single row.

6. **This document becomes history.** Future quarterly reviews will compare to this one. Keep them all. Trend data over 4–6 quarters reveals what's working.
