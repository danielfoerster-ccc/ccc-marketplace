## Phase 1: Administrative Setup (Days 1–3)

**Target:** Complete within 3 days of contract signature. Ensures payment is locked and internal systems are ready.

### Step 1: Contract Signed & Filed

**What to do:**
- Receive final signed contract from client
- Store in: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/Contracts/[Client Name] - CCC Engagement Contract - YYYY-MM-DD.pdf`
- Keep a copy in your CRM as well

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Same day contract is signed  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 2: Confirm Payment Method & Schedule

**What to do:**
- Call or email client: "Which payment method works best for you? Wire, ACH, credit card, or other?"
- Document their choice and expected timeline
- Provide clear payment instructions (IBAN, wire details, etc.)

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Within 24 hours of signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 3: Issue Onboarding Invoice

**What to do:**
- Create invoice for engagement fee (typically €1,500–3,500)
- Terms: Due within 5 days
- Include payment instructions and deadline
- Add to your accounting system

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Within 48 hours of signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 4: Confirm Payment Received

**What to do:**
- Monitor for payment in your bank account
- Mark as "Paid" in your accounting system
- Send thank-you email to client: "Payment received. We're moving to data setup."

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Within 7 days of invoice (escalate if delayed)  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 5: Create Internal Project File

**What to do:**
- Create client folder in your project management system: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/`
- Create subfolders: `Contracts/`, `Reports/`, `Meeting Notes/`, `Data/`, `Tracking/`
- Create master engagement document (see template below) with:
  - Client profile (name, business type, revenue, location, key contacts)
  - Engagement dates (start, assessment, report, quarterly reviews)
  - Success definition (what does "success" look like?)
  - Communication preferences (email, phone, Zoom, timezone)
  - Financial data location

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 2–3 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

**Phase 1 Checkpoint:** By end of Day 3, you should have:
- ✓ Contract signed & filed
- ✓ Payment method confirmed & invoice sent
- ✓ Payment received (or clearly scheduled)
- ✓ Internal project file created

## Phase 2: Access Provisioning (Days 3–10)

**Target:** Complete within 10 days of signature. Ensures you have all financial data needed for assessment.

**Critical rule:** No assessment begins until you have 12+ months of bank statements AND accounting software access confirmed. Period.

### Step 6: Request Data Access & Accounting Software Login

**What to do:**
- Send email to client with this exact list (see Email Templates):

> I need access to:
> 1. Your accounting software (DATEV / Lexoffice / QuickBooks / etc.)
>    - Username & password OR invite link
> 2. 12+ months of bank statements (exported from your bank)
> 3. P&L for the past 3 years (if available)
> 4. Any prior tax returns (last 2 years, if comfortable sharing)
>
> Timeline: Send by [DATE, typically within 2 days]
> I'll keep this secure and will delete after assessment.

- Be specific. Don't say "send me all your financial documents" (client gets overwhelmed, sends random files)

**Action owner:** You (request), Client (provide)  
**Target:** Request sent Day 3, data provided by Day 7  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 7: Confirm Accounting Software Access

**What to do:**
- Client provides login credentials or invite link
- You log in and test access (can you see bank accounts? P&L? Transaction history?)
- Document the software type (DATEV, Lexoffice, QuickBooks, Xero, etc.)
- Document account structure (how many accounts, what are they for?)
- Screenshot the account list for reference

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Within 48 hours of client sending credentials  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 8: Request & Receive Bank Statements

**What to do:**
- Ask client: "Please export 12+ months of bank statements as PDF or CSV. Include all accounts: operating, payroll, reserve, credit card, etc."
- Receive and verify:
  - All 12 months present (or at least 12+ consecutive months)
  - All accounts represented
  - Dates are clear (Jan 2025 – Dec 2025, or Jan 2024 – Dec 2024, etc.)
- Store in: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/Data/Bank Statements/`

**Action owner:** Client (provide), You (receive & verify)  
**Target:** Day 7–10 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 9: Collect P&L & Financial Statements

**What to do:**
- Request from client: P&L for past 3 years (minimum: last 12 months)
- Alternative: If client has accounting access, you can export the P&L yourself
- Verify: At least 12 months available, clearly labeled by year/period, readable format
- Store in: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/Data/Financial Statements/`

**Action owner:** Client (provide) OR You (extract from software)  
**Target:** Day 7–10 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 10: Collect Prior Tax Returns (If Available)

**What to do:**
- Ask client: "Can you share your last 2 years of tax returns? I'll use them to verify revenue and understand any special circumstances."
- Note: This is optional. Some clients won't have them readily available.
- If available, store in: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/Data/Tax Returns/` (encrypt or restrict access)

**Action owner:** Client (provide), You (receive if provided)  
**Target:** Day 10–14 after signature (not critical path)  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 11: Document Current Account Structure

**What to do:**
- Log in to client's accounting software
- Map out current account structure: What's each account for? How much sits in each?
- Create simple diagram:

```
[Client Name] Account Structure — [DATE]

Operating Account: [Bank name] [Account #]
  Purpose: Daily transactions, payroll
  Typical balance: €[X]

Tax Account (if exists): [Bank name] [Account #]
  Purpose: Reserved for taxes
  Typical balance: €[X]

Profit Account (if exists): [Bank name] [Account #]
  Purpose: Reserved for profit/owner draw
  Typical balance: €[X]

Credit Card: [Card type] [Last 4 digits]
  Purpose: Business expenses
  Typical balance: €[X]

Savings/Reserve: [Bank name] [Account #]
  Purpose: Emergency fund or short-term savings
  Typical balance: €[X]

Other: [Describe]
```

- Store in: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/Data/[Client Name] - Account Structure - Current.md`

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 10 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

**Phase 2 Checkpoint:** By end of Day 10, you should have:
- ✓ Accounting software access confirmed (you tested the login)
- ✓ 12+ months of statements received and verified
- ✓ P&L data collected (minimum 12 months)
- ✓ Account structure documented
- ✓ Tax returns collected (if available)

**If any of these are missing:** Escalate to client immediately. "I need [X] to move forward. Can you send by [SPECIFIC DATE]?" Don't proceed to Phase 3 until data is complete.

## Phase 3: Scheduling (Days 5–14)

**Target:** Complete within 14 days. All 7 meetings locked on calendar.

**Note:** Begin Phase 3 while Phase 2 is still in progress. Scheduling and data collection can happen in parallel.

### Step 12: Schedule Kick-Off Meeting

**What to do:**
- Purpose: Introduce yourself, explain engagement, answer questions, confirm expectations
- Duration: 45–60 minutes
- Timing: Within 2 weeks of signature
- Attendees: Primary decision-maker (required), finance person (recommended)
- Format: Zoom
- Action:
  - Email client: "Does [DATE] [TIME] work for a 60-minute kick-off call?" (get confirmation before sending calendar invite)
  - Send calendar invite with Zoom link
  - Note agenda in invite: "We'll walk through your engagement timeline, I'll explain what I'll be analyzing, and you can ask any questions."
  - Confirm attendance within 24 hours

**Action owner:** You (send), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** Invite sent Day 5, meeting locked by Day 7  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 13: Schedule Assessment Session

**What to do:**
- Purpose: Data deep-dive; understand business, current financial situation, goals
- Duration: 90–120 minutes
- Timing: 1–2 weeks after kick-off
- Attendees: Primary decision-maker (required), finance person (required), CFO/bookkeeper (optional)
- Format: Zoom
- Agenda in invite:
  ```
  1. Business overview (where revenue comes from, growth)
  2. Review account structure & allocation patterns
  3. Identify pain points & goals
  4. Walk through 12 Profit Points assessment together
  5. Confirm next steps & report timeline
  ```
- Action:
  - Confirm availability before sending invite
  - Send calendar invite
  - Get confirmation within 24 hours

**Action owner:** You (send), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** Invite sent Day 7, meeting locked by Day 10  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 14: Schedule Report Walkthrough Meeting

**What to do:**
- Purpose: Present findings, review recommendations, lock first-quarter actions
- Duration: 90 minutes
- Timing: 1–2 weeks after assessment
- Attendees: Primary decision-maker (required), finance person (required)
- Format: Zoom (record for archive)
- Agenda:
  ```
  1. Executive summary of findings
  2. Deep dive into each Profit Point
  3. Identified gaps & opportunities
  4. Proposed first-quarter actions
  5. Q&A
  ```
- Action:
  - Send tentative invite (date will be confirmed once report is near completion)
  - Get initial confirmation; lock date within 48 hours of report completion

**Action owner:** You (send), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** Tentative invite sent Day 10, confirmed by Day 17  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 15: Schedule Quarterly Review #1

**What to do:**
- Purpose: Review first-quarter results vs. targets, celebrate wins, adjust plan
- Duration: 90 minutes
- Timing: 3 months after report walkthrough
- Format: Zoom
- Action:
  - Send tentative invite
  - Get confirmation
  - Lock date

**Action owner:** You (send), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** Tentative invite sent Day 14  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 16: Schedule Quarterly Reviews #2, #3, #4

**What to do:**
- Purpose: Same as Q1 — quarterly progress check, adjustment, accountability
- Duration: 90 minutes each
- Timing: 3 months apart (Month 8–9, 11–12, 14–15 of engagement)
- Format: Zoom
- Action:
  - Send tentative calendar invites for all 4 (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)
  - Get confirmation from client
  - Lock dates on your calendar

**Action owner:** You (send), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** All 4 tentatively scheduled by Day 14  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 17: Confirm All Meeting Dates with Client

**What to do:**
- Send master calendar email summarizing every meeting:

> ENGAGEMENT TIMELINE — [Client Name]
>
> ✓ Kick-off: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> ✓ Assessment: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> ✓ Report Walkthrough: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> ✓ Quarterly Review #1: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> ✓ Quarterly Review #2: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> ✓ Quarterly Review #3: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> ✓ Quarterly Review #4: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
>
> All meetings are locked on your calendar. We'll send Zoom links 24 hours before each call. If you need to reschedule, let me know at least 7 days in advance.

- Get written confirmation from client (reply with "Confirmed" or similar)

**Action owner:** You (send), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** Day 12–14  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 18: Create & Populate Master Calendar

**What to do:**
- In your CRM or shared calendar system, create master view showing:
  - All client meetings (kick-off, assessment, report, Q1–Q4)
  - Your prep time (block time before each call, during report writing, etc.)
  - Deliverable deadlines (when report must be complete, etc.)
  - Key milestones (end of Month 1, Q1, etc.)
- Share calendar with client if using shared platform (Google Calendar, Notion, etc.)

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 14  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 19: Send Meeting Logistics Email

**What to do:**
- Send clear instructions for each upcoming meeting type (customize for each)

Example for kick-off:

> **KICK-OFF MEETING LOGISTICS**
> 📅 Date: [DATE] [TIME] [TIMEZONE]
> 🔗 Zoom link: [URL] (sent 24 hours before)
> ⏱️ Duration: 60 minutes
> 📋 What to have ready: Your calendar, any questions about the process
>
> **WHAT I'LL COVER:**
> 1. Your engagement timeline (meetings, report delivery, etc.)
> 2. What I'll analyze (revenue, profit structure, gaps, opportunities)
> 3. What I need from you (data, attendance, implementation effort)
> 4. Your role in this (we work together; I don't make changes without you)
> 5. Success definition (what "good" looks like by end of Year 1)

- Repeat for each meeting type (assessment, report walkthrough, quarterly reviews)

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 5–7 (kick-off), then as each meeting approaches  
**Status:** ☐

**Phase 3 Checkpoint:** By end of Day 14, you should have:
- ✓ Kick-off meeting scheduled & confirmed (Week 2–3)
- ✓ Assessment meeting scheduled & confirmed (Week 3–4)
- ✓ Report walkthrough scheduled & confirmed (Week 5–6)
- ✓ All 4 quarterly reviews scheduled & tentatively confirmed
- ✓ Master calendar created and shared
- ✓ Client has clear meeting logistics for each call

## Phase 4: Expectations & Prep (Days 7–14)

**Target:** Complete within 14 days. Client fully prepared; engagement is "ready for assessment."

**Note:** Execute these steps in parallel with Phases 2 & 3. They overlap.

### Step 20: Send Engagement Timeline Overview

**What to do:**
- Email client a one-pager showing the full engagement arc:

> **YOUR ENGAGEMENT TIMELINE**
>
> **WEEK 1–2: Data Setup**
> - You: Send me accounting access + 12 months statements
> - Me: Verify data, confirm account structure, schedule meetings
>
> **WEEK 2–3: Kick-Off Call**
> - We meet to align on goals, timeline, and expectations
>
> **WEEK 3–4: Financial Assessment**
> - Me: Analyze your data, assess all 12 Profit Points, calculate Bleed
> - You: Prepare to discuss business history, revenue sources, pain points
>
> **WEEK 5–6: Report Walkthrough**
> - Me: Deliver 18+ page Profit Strategy Assessment Report + presentation
> - You: Review findings, lock in first-quarter actions
>
> **WEEK 6+: Execution & Accountability**
> - You: Implement first-quarter actions
> - Me: Weekly email check-ins, monthly deep-dives if needed
>
> **MONTH 3, 6, 9, 12: Quarterly Reviews**
> - We review results, celebrate wins, adjust plan for next quarter

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 5–7 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 21: Document Client Responsibilities

**What to do:**
- Send client clear list of what YOU expect from THEM:

> **YOUR RESPONSIBILITIES DURING THIS ENGAGEMENT**
>
> **DATA ACCESS & INFORMATION:**
> ☐ Provide accounting software login within 2 days
> ☐ Export + send 12 months of bank statements by [DATE]
> ☐ Provide P&L for past 3 years by [DATE]
> ☐ Be available for questions about your account structure
>
> **MEETING ATTENDANCE:**
> ☐ Attend all scheduled meetings (kick-off, assessment, report, quarterly reviews)
> ☐ Bring primary decision-maker + finance person to each meeting
> ☐ Give 7 days notice if you need to reschedule
>
> **IMPLEMENTATION:**
> ☐ Execute first-quarter actions (account setup, allocation changes)
> ☐ Send me weekly allocation updates starting [DATE]
> ☐ Flag blockers or questions within 24 hours
>
> **COMMUNICATION:**
> ☐ Reply to my emails within 24–48 hours
> ☐ Use [email/WhatsApp/Slack] as primary channel — I check daily
> ☐ If traveling or unavailable, let me know in advance

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 5–7 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 22: Define Success Metrics

**What to do:**
- Work with client to define: What does success look like?
- Examples:
  - "By end of Year 1, we've closed the Bleed completely + increased profit margin by 8 percentage points"
  - "Tax allocation is now accurate. No surprises at year-end."
  - "Monthly profit is up €2,000. I can take home more while maintaining emergency reserves."
  - "Account structure is clean. I understand where every dollar goes."
- Document their top 3 success metrics in writing
- Store in client's master engagement file

**Action owner:** You (discuss), Client (articulate)  
**Target:** Day 7–10 (usually comes up in kick-off meeting)  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 23: Confirm Communication Preferences

**What to do:**
- Ask and document:
  - Primary contact: [Name, title, email, phone]
  - Secondary contact: [If different person for finance]
  - Preferred communication: Email? WhatsApp? Phone?
  - Time zones: Yours vs. theirs — what hours work?
  - Response time expectation: "I'll reply within 24 hours. I expect similar from you."
  - Meeting format: Zoom? Phone? In-person?
  - Any accessibility needs or preferences

**Action owner:** You (ask), Client (confirm)  
**Target:** Day 3–5 (during kick-off call prep)  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 24: Share Engagement Agreement/Expectations Document

**What to do:**
- Create a formal one-pager (or attach as PDF) outlining:
  - Scope of work (what CCC will deliver)
  - Client responsibilities (what they provide)
  - Timeline (kick-off → assessment → report → quarterly reviews)
  - Fees & payment (engagement fee, schedule, what's included)
  - Confidentiality (how their data is handled, stored, protected)
  - Success definition (3 metrics you agreed on)
  - Contact & escalation (who to reach if issues arise)
- Send to client for signature or written acknowledgment
- Store in: `03 - OPERATIONS/[Client Name]/Contracts/[Client Name] - Engagement Expectations - YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Action owner:** You (create & send), Client (sign or acknowledge)  
**Target:** Day 7–10  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 25: Explain Your Process (12 Profit Points Framework)

**What to do:**
- Send client high-level overview of what you'll analyze:

> **WHAT I'LL ANALYZE (The 12 Profit Points)**
>
> 1. **Account Structure Alignment** — Do you have the right accounts set up?
> 2. **Allocation Accuracy** — Are you allocating money correctly?
> 3. **Price Integrity** — Are you pricing right for your market?
> 4. **Cost of Delivery (COGS)** — What are you actually spending to deliver?
> 5. **Operating Expense Control** — Are overhead costs optimized?
> 6. **Tax Provisioning** — Are you saving enough for taxes?
> 7. **Profit Extraction** — Are you taking your profit?
> 8. **Debt Service Strategy** — How are you managing debt?
> 9. **Cash Reserve Adequacy** — Do you have an emergency fund?
> 10. **Owner Compensation** — Are you paying yourself fairly?
> 11. **Growth Reinvestment** — Are you reinvesting smart?
> 12. **Financial Visibility** — Can you see what's happening real-time?
>
> Each point gets scored 1–10. My job is to find your lowest scores and give you a roadmap to fix them.
> This is not a tax audit or business audit. This is a profit architecture assessment.

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 5–7 after signature  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 26: Set Expectations for Report Delivery

**What to do:**
- Communicate clearly:
  - When you'll deliver the report (typically 1–2 weeks after assessment)
  - What format (PDF document, email, printed)
  - How it will be presented (live walkthrough via Zoom, no surprises)
  - How long they'll have to review before walkthrough (24–48 hours to read Executive Summary)

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 7–10  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 27: Explain Quarterly Review Process

**What to do:**
- Help client understand what quarterly reviews are:

> **QUARTERLY REVIEWS (Every 3 months)**
>
> **What we do:**
> 1. Review results vs. targets (hitting projections?)
> 2. Celebrate wins (what went well?)
> 3. Diagnose gaps (where did we miss, and why?)
> 4. Adjust next quarter's plan
> 5. Lock in next quarter's actions
>
> **What you bring:**
> - Updated financial data (P&L, account balances)
> - Implementation updates (which actions did you complete?)
> - Questions or blockers
>
> **What I bring:**
> - Trend analysis (how are we trending vs. targets?)
> - Recommendations (should we adjust?)
> - Next quarter's specific actions
>
> **Duration:** 90 minutes | **Frequency:** Every 3 months | **Timeline:** Month 3, 6, 9, 12+

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 10–14  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 28: Clarify What Changes & When

**What to do:**
- Explain timing realistically:
  - "Week 1: You'll open new accounts and set up structure. Zero cost, low effort."
  - "Weeks 2–4: You'll adjust allocation percentages. Takes 10 minutes/week."
  - "Month 2–3: You'll implement strategic recommendations. This is where the work lives."
  - "Month 3+: Behavioral change. Discipline. Checking balances weekly, not quarterly."
- Set expectations: "This isn't a quick fix. It's a 6–12 month transformation. But the first wins come in Month 1."

**Action owner:** You (kick-off call)  
**Target:** Day 7–10  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 29: Explain Your Fee Structure & What's Included

**What to do:**
- Be transparent about pricing (should already be in proposal):
  - Engagement fee: [€X]
  - What's included: Assessment, report, first quarterly review, email support
  - What's not: Implementation (client does this), accounting software (client pays)
  - Optional: Ongoing monthly coaching/execution support (additional fee)

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 1–2 (should already be in proposal)  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 30: Set Up Secure Data Storage

**What to do:**
- Confirm and document your data security practices:
  - Where you store client data (encrypted drive, secure server, etc.)
  - How long you keep it (typically 2 years after engagement ends)
  - Who has access (you only, unless you have a VA with NDA)
  - How client can delete data (just ask)
- Include privacy assurance in writing

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 5–7  
**Status:** ☐

### Step 31: Create Welcome Packet

**What to do:**
- Compile all information from Steps 20–30 into a polished document (PDF or email):
  - Engagement timeline overview
  - Client responsibilities checklist
  - Success metrics (agreed-upon)
  - Communication preferences (confirmed)
  - Engagement expectations document (signed)
  - Process overview (12 Profit Points)
  - Report delivery timeline
  - Quarterly review explanation
  - Fee structure breakdown
  - Data security & privacy assurance
  - Your contact info + emergency contact
  - Next steps (kick-off call date/time/link)
- Send as polished email or PDF attachment

**Action owner:** You  
**Target:** Day 10–14  
**Status:** ☐

**Action owner:** You

**Status:** ☐

## Common Blockers & Fixes

### Blocker 1: Client doesn't respond to data request

**Symptom:** You email asking for accounting software access and bank statements. Radio silence for 3–5 days.

**Root cause:** Email alone is too easy to ignore. Client is busy or overwhelmed by the request.

**Fix:**
- Call them instead of emailing. "Hi [Name], I sent a data request. When can you get me those statements?" Speaking to them directly creates urgency.
- Offer to call their accountant/bookkeeper with them. Remove the friction.
- If they're still slow, ask: "Is this the right time for an engagement? What would make this easier?" (May need to pause.)

### Blocker 2: Client only sends partial data

**Symptom:** Client sends 6 months of statements instead of 12. Or P&L without bank statements.

**Root cause:** Client doesn't understand why you need all of it. Requested too broadly ("send all your financial documents").

**Fix:**
- Follow up with specific list: "I have 6 months; I need all 12. Here's why: [one sentence on why 12 months matters]."
- Be patient but firm. "I can't move forward with assessment until I have 12 months. Can you get me the rest by [DATE]?"

### Blocker 3: Accounting software access is denied

**Symptom:** Client says "My accountant won't let me give you access" or "I don't know the password."

**Root cause:** Trust issue or technical limitation.

**Fix:**
- Ask client to contact their accountant: "Can you ask [Accountant Name] to grant me read-only access to [Software]? I'll sign a confidentiality agreement if needed."
- Alternative: Ask client to export P&L and bank statements directly instead of giving access. Not ideal (you prefer live access), but workable.
- If they absolutely can't provide access, red flag. This is a warning sign about data transparency. Ask: "How will you track financial progress after our engagement?" If they're resistant to transparency, may not be a good fit.

### Blocker 4: Client wants to skip the kick-off meeting

**Symptom:** Client says "Can we skip the kick-off and go straight to assessment? I'm busy."

**Root cause:** Doesn't understand the purpose of kick-off. Wants to "save time."

**Fix:**
- Explain: "The kick-off isn't optional. It's 60 minutes where we align on goals and expectations. Without this, we risk misalignment later."
- Reschedule instead of skipping. "I understand you're busy. What week works better for you?"
- If they insist on skipping, document it. You've warned them about misalignment risk.

### Blocker 5: Client can't attend assessment meeting (only bookkeeper available)

**Symptom:** Client says "Can [Bookkeeper] join instead? I'm traveling that week."

**Root cause:** Client doesn't understand that the decision-maker needs to be present.

**Fix:**
- Push back, firmly but politely: "I need you on this call. The assessment includes discussing your business goals, profit targets, and what changes you want to make. Only you can answer those questions. Can we reschedule so you're available?"
- If rescheduling is impossible, pause assessment. "Let's move this to [DATE] when you're back. No point assessing if you can't be present."

### Blocker 6: Client misses scheduled meeting with no notice

**Symptom:** You show up to the kick-off/assessment call. Client doesn't.

**Root cause:** Forgot, or something came up.

**Fix:**
- Send a reminder 24 hours before each call. "Kick-off tomorrow at [TIME]. Zoom link: [URL]."
- If client misses: Call them immediately. "I was waiting for you. Everything OK?" Don't reschedule via email — confirm on the phone.
- If this happens more than once, it's a red flag. Client may not be serious about the engagement. Address it directly: "I'm noticing we're having trouble connecting. What's going on?"

### Blocker 7: Client wants to extend onboarding beyond 14 days

**Symptom:** It's Day 15 and you're still on Phase 2 (data collection). Client says "Can we take more time?"

**Root cause:** Slow data access or client distracted with other priorities.

**Fix:**
- Understand the blockers. "What's holding us up? Is it data from your accountant? Or something else?"
- Set a hard deadline. "Let's lock in [DATE] as our final onboarding completion date. After that, we move to assessment regardless."
- If client consistently misses deadlines, escalate: "I need to know if you're serious about this engagement. If not, we can pause and restart when you're ready."

### Blocker 8: Client won't commit to quarterly reviews

**Symptom:** Client says "Let's skip Q2 and Q3 reviews. We can just do Q1 and Q4."

**Root cause:** Doesn't see value in quarterly reviews. Wants to minimize cost/time.

**Fix:**
- Explain the value: "Quarterly reviews keep us accountable. Without them, momentum dies. You'll skip implementing actions, and by Q4 you'll be off track."
- It's non-negotiable. "All 4 quarterly reviews are part of your engagement. They're already scheduled."
- If client still pushes back, note it. May indicate lack of commitment to implementation.

### Blocker 9: Client says "We need the assessment done faster. Can we compress the timeline?"

**Symptom:** Client wants assessment to happen in Week 2 instead of Week 4.

**Root cause:** Impatience or external business pressure.

**Fix:**
- Explain why timeline matters: "Assessment requires complete data (12 months of statements) + kick-off alignment. I can't skip those. But if you can get me data by [EARLY DATE], we can accelerate to [EARLY ASSESSMENT DATE]."
- Don't compromise data quality for speed. Wrong recommendations made quickly are worse than right recommendations made slowly.

## Final Notes

- **All 32 steps must be completed before assessment.** No exceptions. Missing steps = incomplete picture = wrong analysis.
- **Data quality is the #1 risk.** Incomplete statements, missing months, or accounting access issues will delay everything. Prioritize this.
- **Scheduling all quarterly reviews upfront prevents momentum loss.** Don't skip this step.
- **Document everything in writing.** Emails, calendar invites, confirmation messages. Paper trail matters.
- **Use the templates.** They're battle-tested. Personalize but don't reinvent.

Good luck with your engagements.
