# Workshop Preparation Guide — Cashflow Control System

Complete reference for preparing a Cashflow Control workshop. Use this alongside the [[SOP Workshop Delivery Preparation — CCC]] for detailed timing and narrative instructions.

---

## Pre-Workshop Checklist (2 Weeks Before)

- [ ] Send Module 0 discovery questions to participants (10–12 days before)
- [ ] Collect responses (expect 70–80% response by day 7)
- [ ] Analyze responses: identify top 3 pain points, common revenue range, success definitions
- [ ] Customize opening hook (based on Module 0 data)
- [ ] Select top 3 stories to tell (match to participant pain)
- [ ] Prepare M&M simulation materials (live or webinar version)
- [ ] Create presentation slides (15–20 slides, one idea per slide)
- [ ] Prepare materials & logistics (tech testing, room setup, supplies)
- [ ] Full rehearsal run-through (60 min with timer)
- [ ] Confidence check: 8+/10 readiness

## Story Selection Framework

**Map your stories to their pain. If story doesn't match pain, don't use it.**

### Story Structure:
1. **Situation** — "I was in your chair. Doing €XXK revenue."
2. **Problem** — "I had X challenge." [Match to Module 0]
3. **Turning point** — "Then I realized..."
4. **Solution moment** — "I figured out..."
5. **Result** — "Now I have..."

### Example: Tax Story

```
SITUATION:
"5 years ago, I was running €3,000/month revenue. 
I had revenue, but no visibility into profit."

PROBLEM (match to Module 0):
"I hadn't set aside anything for taxes. September hits, 
I get a tax bill for €8,000. I didn't have it. 
Had to take a loan. It was humiliating."

TURNING POINT:
"That's when I realized: I didn't have a money problem. 
I had an architecture problem."

SOLUTION:
"A consultant showed me: You need to allocate revenue 
the moment it lands. 22% goes to tax. Pre-allocated. 
No surprises."

RESULT:
"Now? Haven't had a tax surprise in 6 years. 
I know what I owe in June. No panic in September."

EMOTIONAL CORE: Recognition (they've felt this) 
→ Relief (there's a fix) → Confidence (you survived it)
```

## M&M Simulation Setup Guide

**This is the most important 10 minutes of the workshop. Allocate full time. Rehearse it.**

### Materials Needed

**For live in-person:**
- 1 large bag of M&Ms (or similar small candy)
- 6–8 small plates, cups, or bowls (one per "account")
- Whiteboard or large paper (to track allocation percentages)
- Example: Operating 30%, Tax 22%, Profit 15%, Owner Draw 28%, Debt 5%

**For webinar:**
- Animated slides showing M&Ms (or coins) moving between accounts
- OR: Ask participants to grab coins/candy from home and follow along
- Slides with pie charts showing current vs. ideal allocation

### 10-Minute Simulation Script

**[Note: This is the delivery script, not the prep outline. Rehearse this exactly.]**

```
"We're going to do something that changes how you see your money. 
This is the M&M Simulation.

Here's what's happening:
- These M&Ms represent your monthly revenue. Let's say you bring in €5,000.
- I'm dividing these M&Ms into accounts:
  1. Operating (cost to deliver your service)
  2. Tax (what you owe government)
  3. Profit (what you keep)
  4. Owner compensation (your salary)
  5. Debt service (if you have loans)

Most of you don't have this set up. You get revenue in one account, 
and it's chaos. Let me show you what that looks like.

[DUMP ALL M&Ms INTO ONE PILE. POINT AT IT.]

CHAOS. Everything mixed. You pay yourself randomly. 
You panic on tax day. Sound familiar?

Now, watch what happens when we design allocation.

CURRENT STATE [based on their data]:
Operating: 30% (you told me)
Tax: 5% (you said — this is the problem)
Profit: 0% (you said)
Owner draw: 65% (whatever's left)

[SEPARATE M&Ms INTO THESE PILES]

But SHOULD be:
Operating: 35%
Tax: 22%
Profit: 15%
Owner draw: 28%

[MOVE M&Ms TO CORRECT PROPORTIONS]

See the gap? That gap is profit leaking. That's the Bleed.

[PAUSE. LET IT SINK IN.]

When you allocate right, profit stops hiding. 
It becomes visible. It becomes predictable. 
And it's YOURS.

By the end of today, you'll understand exactly 
how to do this with your own money."
```

### Timing & Pacing

- **Setup (1 min):** Explain the concept
- **Show chaos (2 min):** Dump all M&Ms, point, let them feel the mess
- **Show current state (3 min):** Separate into their actual allocation
- **Show ideal state (3 min):** Reorganize into what it should be
- **Land the insight (1 min):** "Profit stops hiding."

**Total: 10 minutes. Don't rush.**

### Success Indicators
- Participants lean in (curious)
- You see understanding clicks (head nods, "oh")
- Someone asks "How do I set this up?" (perfect — now they're hooked)
- Recording captures the M&M piles (visual proof for follow-up)

## Presentation Slide Structure (15–20 Slides)

**One idea per slide. Visuals only. You talk; slide reinforces.**

| # | Title | Content | Notes |
|---|-------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Title Slide | Workshop name, your name, date | Professional, clean |
| 2 | Opening Statement | Customized hook (from participant analysis) | Most important slide — lands emotional resonance |
| 3–4 | Challenge Identification | 3–4 statements from Module 0 ("I don't know profit", "Tax surprises", etc.) | Make them feel seen |
| 5 | Your Struggle | Photo or visual + 1 sentence ("5 years ago, I didn't know where my profit went") | Vulnerability |
| 6 | The Turning Point | Visual + 1 sentence ("Then I learned: allocation is the architecture") | Hope moment |
| 7–8 | Results Showcase | Before/after visuals (profit margins, tax planning, cash visibility) | Credibility |
| 9 | Old World | Visual of chaos (1 account, random spending, surprises) | Show the pain |
| 10 | New World | Visual of clarity (5 accounts, allocation %, predictability) | Show the solution |
| 11 | The 9-Step System | Numbered list (1. Audit Current State, 2. Define Target Allocation, etc.) | Overview only |
| 12 | M&M Simulation Preview | Pie chart showing current vs. ideal allocation | Sets up the physical demo |
| 13–14 | 12 Profit Points Framework | Intro slide (will be covered in assessment) | Position for next step |
| 15 | Common Mistakes | "Don't do these 6 things" (list only, you'll explain) | Prevention angle |
| 16 | First Steps | Timeline: Day 1 (calculate), Day 3 (call accountant), Day 5 (open accounts), Week 2 (start allocation) | Momentum |
| 17 | Call to Action | Large button/graphic: "Ready? Book a discovery call" + [LINK] | Conversion point |
| 18 | Closing | Simple statement: "You showed up. That matters. Now take the next step." | Inspirational close |

**Design rules:**
- No bullet points (makes you read them, kills engagement)
- Big text (40+ point font, readable on Zoom or back of room)
- Visuals only (pie charts, before/after photos, client testimonials)
- One sentence max per slide (you're the voice)
- No more than 20 slides (respect time)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Generic opening (instead of customized) | Audience doesn't feel seen. "This person doesn't get my pain." | Always customize from Module 0 data. Use their exact words: "60% of you said..." |
| Skipping M&M simulation | Workshop becomes lecture. Engagement drops 40%. | M&M is non-negotiable. Full 10 minutes. Visceral, memorable. |
| Running over time, cutting CTA | You deliver 90 min content in 60 min. CTA is rushed or skipped. | Rehearse with timer. If long, cut/condense low-engagement sections. Protect CTA. |
| Weak CTA ("If anyone wants to book...") | Wimpy tone. No urgency. 5% book. | Strong CTA: "Interested? Book now. [LINK]. I'll see you there." Assume yes. |
| Not tracking engagement | Workshop ends. You don't know who was most engaged. Follow-up is random. | Keep scorecard visible. Score during (not after). +1 per question, participation, visibility. |
| Waiting 3+ days to follow up | Momentum dies. People forget. Conversion drops 50%+. | Same-day recap (within 24h). Personalized follow-up within 48h. Speed matters. |
| Generic follow-up email | Feels robotic. "This is junk mail." No conversion. | Personalized to top 5 engagers. Reference their specific questions/comments. 3x higher conversion. |
| Reading slides instead of talking | Boring. Disconnected. Audience zones out. | Slides = visual support only. YOU talk. Make eye contact. Smile. Energy > words. |
| Not collecting feedback | Workshop ends. You don't know what worked. No testimonials. | Same email as follow-up: "Rate today: 9–10 / 7–8 / 5–6?" + "One thing I'll take: ___" 30% respond. |

## Quick Reference: 9-Step Arc at a Glance

1. **Introduction** (5 min) — "I'm a peer who figured this out."
2. **Challenge** (10 min) — Name their pain from Module 0.
3. **My Struggle** (10 min) — Vulnerable story matching their pain.
4. **Solution** (10 min) — "I found a way. It's simple."
5. **Results** (10 min) — Proof (your results + 2–3 client examples).
6. **Old World vs. New World** (15 min) — Reframe their thinking.
7. **System Explained + M&M** (20 min) — 9 steps + 10-min M&M simulation.
8. **Mistakes** (15 min) — 6 common traps to avoid.
9. **CTA** (10 min) — "Book a discovery call. Here's the link."

**Total: 60–70 minutes (includes Q&A buffer)**
