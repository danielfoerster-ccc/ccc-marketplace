# ccc-cashflow-workshop-prep

**Workflow: Analyze → Customize → Prepare → Package.** Takes Module 0 discovery responses from workshop participants, synthesizes insights, and produces a complete customized workshop delivery kit.

This skill PREPARES workshops. The consultant delivers them live. Every output here (talking points, M&M script, follow-up sequences, engagement scorecards) supports live delivery.

## Core Workshop Structure

The Cashflow Control workshop follows Daniel Förster's **9-Step Narrative Arc**, a proven story structure that moves participants from "I don't know if this applies" → "This is exactly what I need" → "I'm booking a discovery call":

1. **Introduction** — Credibility. "I'm a peer. I've figured this out."
2. **Challenge Identification** — Resonance. Name their pain.
3. **My Struggle** — Connection. Vulnerability.
4. **Finding the Solution** — Hope. "I found a way. It's simple."
5. **Results Showcase** — Proof. "Other people have done it."
6. **Old World vs. New World** — Reframe. "You're thinking about this wrong."
7. **The System Explained** — Education. The 5-account allocation structure + **M&M Simulation** (experiential proof).
8. **Common Mistakes** — Prevention. Avoid these traps.
9. **First Steps + CTA** — Momentum. "Book a discovery call."

The **M&M Simulation** is the emotional centerpiece — without it, the workshop is just a lecture. Participants physically separate M&Ms (representing revenue) into piles: Operating, Tax, Profit, Owner Draw, Debt Service. Visual, tactile, memorable.

## M&M Simulation — Detailed Script

**Duration:** 15 minutes (setup, Round 1, Round 2, debrief, discussion)

**Materials per participant:**
- 1 large plate (represents business bank account)
- 1 Solo cup or small cup (represents personal bank account)
- 4 small plates (for Round 2 allocations)
- 1 pen
- ~50 M&Ms or chocolates (from central bowl, distributed during rounds)
- 5 post-it notes or labels (Income, Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, OpEx)

**Setup (2 min):**
Divide participants into two groups: "Small Business" (plain white plates for lighter expense scenarios) and "Established Business" (patterned plates for heavier expense loads). This surface-level difference creates immediate visual distinction and primes the teaching moment: same revenue, different overhead burden.

**Round 1: Bank Balance Accounting — The Old Way (6 min)**

Setup the scenario:
- Large plate = business bank account
- Solo cup = personal bank account
- Everyone starts with 30 M&Ms (revenue for Q1)

Walk through 3 months of transactions:
- Month 1: Income (place 10 M&Ms on large plate). Rent comes due ($2, remove 2 M&Ms). Payroll ($3, remove 3). Owner's pay from "whatever's left" (distribute roughly half, move to Solo cup).
- Month 2: More income (10 M&Ms added). New client! But office chair breaks (unexpected $1 expense). Owner takes a vacation — pays personal expenses from business account ($2 from the cup).
- Month 3: Income arrives (10 M&Ms). But end-of-quarter tax bill is looming. "How much do you have left for taxes?" (Silence. Most have 3-5 M&Ms. Tax needs 8-10.)

**Key teaching moment (pause here):**
- "This is what we call **bank balance accounting** — you spend based on what's in the account, not what's actually available."
- "How many of you check your bank balance before making a business purchase?" (Hands go up.)
- "How many have had to pay taxes from personal savings?" (More hands.)
- "That's what happened to you just now. No one planned for it. You just kept spending."

**Round 2: Profit First — The New Way (6 min)**

Reset: Everyone gets 30 M&Ms again.

New structure: Add 4 small plates, labeled:
- **Income** (incoming M&Ms land here first)
- **Profit** (10% allocation — non-negotiable)
- **Owner's Pay** (25% allocation)
- **Tax** (15% allocation)
- **OpEx** (50% allocation — this is what you actually spend)

Same 3 months, same expenses, same income:
- Month 1: Income arrives (10 M&Ms → Income plate). Allocate FIRST: 1 to Profit, 2.5 to Owner's Pay, 1.5 to Tax, 5 to OpEx. Now spend from OpEx only: rent (2), payroll (3). You have $0 left in OpEx. Chair breaks — you can't afford it.

**Key teaching moment:**
- "The chair is broken. What do you do? In the old way, you'd pay from the business account or personal savings. In Profit First, you **can't afford it** because OpEx is empty."
- "That's the entire point. You're not 'disciplined' — the system makes the decision for you."

- Month 2: Income (10 M&Ms → Income). Allocate (1 Profit, 2.5 Owner's Pay, 1.5 Tax, 5 OpEx). Spend OpEx (2 rent, 3 payroll = fully allocated). Personal vacation comes up — you can use Owner's Pay for that.
- Month 3: Same allocation. End of quarter comes: **Tax plate has 4.5 M&Ms**. You can't pay full tax, but you have something. More importantly, **Profit plate has 3 M&Ms**. 

**Celebration moment (permission-based):**
- "In the old way, you had no profit and no tax reserve. In Profit First, after 3 months of the exact same revenue and expenses, you have built profit and a tax reserve. Eat half the M&Ms from your Profit plate. You earned them."
- (Participants actually eat the M&Ms. Celebration. Relief.)

**Debrief Questions (1 min):**
- "What's the difference between accounting profit and cash profit?" (Accounting profit ≠ cash in your account. Cash discipline creates both.)
- "Why does the system work when willpower doesn't?" (The system removes choice. You can't overspend OpEx when it's empty.)
- "What scares you about this approach?" (Listen. Often: "Payroll might be higher some months" or "What if there's an emergency?" This opens into the Debt Service conversation and the Instant Assessment.)

## Alternative Training Format: 6-Module Program

Beyond the 60-minute workshop, Daniel also offers a comprehensive **6-Week Training Program** (4 hours per week, 6 modules, 24 hours total). This deeper dive covers system setup, customization, and ongoing management.

**Module 1 (Week 1): 4 Principles of Profit First**
- Use Small Plates (separate accounts force discipline)
- Serve Sequentially (allocate before spending)
- Remove Temptation (visibility + rules = behavioral change)
- Enforce a Rhythm (weekly accounting, monthly allocations, quarterly reviews)

**Module 2 (Week 2): Assessing Business Health**
- Run the Instant Assessment (revenue targets, allocation percentages, debt analysis)
- Benchmark against industry standards
- Identify biggest cash leaks

**Module 3 (Week 3): The Roll Out Plan**
- 13-week implementation timeline
- Week 1-4: Set up accounts, reconcile chart of accounts
- Week 5-8: Run parallel (old system + Profit First)
- Week 9-13: Full transition, troubleshooting

**Module 4 (Week 4): Customizing Chart of Accounts**
- Beyond the 5-account model (when to add sub-accounts)
- Project-based P&L tracking
- Client profitability analysis

**Module 5 (Week 5): Expense Audit**
- Line-by-line review of historical spending
- Identify non-essential, recurring waste
- Recalculate OpEx baseline and allocations

**Module 6 (Week 6): Running the System**
- Weekly accounting process (30 min)
- Monthly review & reallocation (60 min)
- Quarterly strategy & tax planning (90 min)
- Troubleshooting common issues (off-payroll months, seasonal swings, debt payoff)

This modular approach suits businesses that want to implement Profit First as a foundational operating system, not just a concept.

## 4-Phase Workflow

### Phase 1: Participant Analysis (1–2 hours)

Input: Module 0 pre-survey responses (6 open-ended questions about revenue, pain points, barriers, success definition).

Output: Participant analysis document identifying:
- Common revenue range
- Top 3 pain points (with frequency counts)
- What they've tried before (and why it failed)
- Success definition for this cohort
- Biggest barriers holding them back
- Customization opportunities (which stories/examples will resonate)

**What this enables:** You don't deliver a generic workshop. You customize the opening hook, story selection, and examples to THIS audience.

### Phase 2: Content Customization (2–3 hours)

Input: Participant analysis + your personal stories/client results.

Output: Customized talking points for each of the 9 steps, including:
- **Customized Opening Hook** — Name their pain. "60% of you said X. That's exactly what we're fixing."
- **Top 3 Stories** — Matched to their pain points. If tax surprises are common, tell a tax story.
- **Results Examples** — Pick client/personal results that resonate.
- **M&M Simulation Script** — Full 10-minute script, live and webinar versions.
- **Common Mistakes** — Tailor the 6 mistakes to what they're likely doing.
- **CTA Copy** — Strong, confident call to action (not "if you want to, maybe book a call").

### Phase 3: Materials Package (2–3 hours)

Input: Customized talking points + workshop format (webinar vs. live).

Output: Ready-to-deliver materials:
- Presentation slides (15–20 slides, one idea per slide, visuals only)
- M&M simulation setup checklist (materials needed, timing, transitions)
- Speaking notes (not full script, just reminders + transitions)
- Engagement scorecard (track who asks questions, participates, engages)
- Participant list with revenue ranges (visible during workshop)
- Timer + pacing guide (stay on track, protect the CTA)

**Materials Checklist — Live Workshop Format:**
- M&Ms or chocolates (50–60 per participant; purchase bulk)
- Plates: large (business account), Solo cups or small cups (personal account), 4 small plates per participant (allocations)
- Post-it notes or label stickers (Income, Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, OpEx)
- Pens (1 per participant for note-taking)
- Printed handouts (9-step narrative summary, key allocation percentages, next-steps checklist)
- Projector & screen (for slides and timer visibility)
- Timer or stopwatch (visible to participants; keeps pacing tight)
- Engagement scorecard (printed, 1 per facilitator to track participation)

**Materials Checklist — Webinar Format:**
- Virtual allocation tool (shared spreadsheet or interactive web tool; participants see real-time allocation math)
- Screen share setup (slides + timer visible to all)
- Breakout room access (divide participants into small groups for simulation; alternate: single-group simulation with shared screen)
- Digital handout PDF (emailed pre-workshop; participants download and print locally if preferred)
- Chat monitoring (co-facilitator or assistant tracks engagement, questions, real-time sentiment)
- Recording enabled (with participant consent; send recap link within 24h)

For both formats: Prep a backup plan. Live workshop? Bring extra plates and M&Ms (some get dropped). Webinar? Test spreadsheet/tool 30 min before start; have a PDF print-out version ready if tech fails.

### Phase 4: Follow-Up Design (1–2 hours)

Input: Engagement scores + top 5 engagers identified during workshop preparation.

Output: Personalized follow-up sequence:
- **Day 0 (same day, within 24 hours):** Recap email with key takeaways + booking link
- **Day 3:** Personalized check-in to top 5 engagers (reference their specific questions)
- **Day 7:** Case study or success story email
- **Day 14:** Formal 1:1 implementation offer
- **Day 30:** Final check-in + testimonial request

This is where conversion happens. The workshop is lead gen; the follow-up is where deals close.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Gather Module 0 Responses

Ask the user to provide:
- Pre-survey responses (ideally as a spreadsheet, CSV, or text copy-paste)
- Expected attendee count
- Workshop date and format (webinar/live/hybrid)
- Your (the consultant's) key stories and client results (brief outline)

### Step 2: Run Participant Analysis

Ask clarifying questions if needed:
- "Which of these pain points feel most urgent for your audience?"
- "Do you have a specific client story that maps to [pain point]?"
- "What's your tax rate / typical client allocation percentages?"

Then produce: **Participant Analysis** document.

### Step 3: Customize Content

Walk through each of the 9 steps:
- Show the consultant customized hooks, stories, talking points
- Get approval on tone, examples, positioning
- Refine if needed

Output: **Customized Talking Points** document.

### Step 4: Build Materials Package

Create and assemble:
- Slide deck (export as PDF or shareable link)
- M&M simulation setup guide with timing
- Speaking notes with transitions
- Engagement scorecard template
- Pacing timeline

Output: **Workshop Materials Package** folder with all files ready to use.

### Step 5: Design Follow-Up Sequences

Create:
- Email templates for day 0, 3, 7, 14, 30
- Personalization prompts (reference their question, tell a story, offer a specific help)
- Booking link integration

Output: **Follow-Up Sequence** guide.

### Step 6: Create Workshop Playbook

Compile everything into one master document:
- Pre-workshop checklist (what to confirm 3 days before)
- Day-of setup checklist (15 min before start)
- 9-step delivery script (bullet points, not full script)
- Timing guide (keep to 60 min)
- Engagement tracking instructions
- Post-workshop action items (recap email within 24h, personalized follow-up within 48h)
- Follow-up email templates

Output: **Workshop Playbook** — single document the consultant uses to deliver.

## Rules (Update When Things Go Wrong)

| Situation | Broken Output | Root Cause | Fix |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| Opening hook doesn't land | Audience doesn't feel seen | Generic hook instead of customized | Always customize opening based on Module 0. Use specific pain point language: "60% of you said X." |
| M&M simulation feels rushed | Consultant skips or shortens it | Time pressure or discomfort with "gimmick" | M&M is non-negotiable. It's the most memorable 10 minutes. Allocate full time, rehearse it. |
| No discovery calls booked | CTA is weak or buried | "If anyone wants to book a call..." wimpy tone | Make CTA strong & confident: "Interested? Book now. [LINK]. I'll see you there." Assume yes. |
| Weak follow-up conversion | Generic recap email to 200 people | No personalization, no speed | Personalize top 5 engagers within 48 hours. Reference their specific questions. Generic email goes to everyone same-day; personalized goes to hot leads within 48h. |
| Stories don't resonate | Consultant picks stories they love | Didn't map to participant pain points from Module 0 | Always match stories to Module 0 data. If they said "tax surprises," tell a tax story. |
| Audience disengages mid-workshop | Running over time, cutting Q&A & CTA | Didn't rehearse with timer, tried to fit too much | Rehearse full 60 min with timer. If running long, cut/condense "nice to have" sections (mistakes, context). Protect the CTA. |
| Can't track who engaged | No engagement scorecard during delivery | Forgot to keep score, too focused on slides | Have engagement scorecard visible. +1 point: question asked, participation, visible attention. Score during, not after. |
| Post-workshop stall | Waiting 3+ days to follow up | Momentum died, people forgot | Same-day recap (within 24h). Personalized outreach within 48h. Speed is critical. |

## Self-Improvement

When a consultant completes a workshop and shares results:
- Note which talking points generated the most questions
- Note which stories had the highest engagement (ask the consultant to watch the recording)
- Update "Top 3 Stories" section with winning story angles
- If a customized opening hook generated more engagement than generic, note the specific framing

When follow-up conversion rates differ from 20% target:
- If lower: Review CTA strength, follow-up timing, personalization quality
- If higher: Document what worked (story selection? Opening hook? Follow-up sequence?) and save as a reference example

This skill gets better every time it's used. Treat the outputs as templates, not final — the consultant's real-time adjustments and feedback are how this becomes a repeatable, high-conversion system.
