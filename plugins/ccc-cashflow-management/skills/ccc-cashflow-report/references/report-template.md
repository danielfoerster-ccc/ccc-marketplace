## 3. THE BLEED ANALYSIS

### What Is Bleed? Why It Matters.

**The Bleed** is the gap between what you actually allocate to each account (Tax, Profit, Owner Comp, etc.) and what you *need* to allocate to each account. It's money leaking away silently — month after month, quarter after quarter.

Example: If you need €1,200/month in a tax reserve account but you're only putting in €600/month, your monthly Bleed is €600. Over a year, that's €7,200. Over three years, €21,600. That's real money. And if your tax bill comes due in Month 13, you'll owe the full amount — but you've under-provisioned by €7,200. That creates a cash crisis.

The Bleed happens for a few reasons:
1. **You don't know your true tax obligation** (most common). You guess at a tax %, it's too low, money doesn't get set aside, liability compounds.
2. **Your account structure doesn't support the allocations you know you need.** You know you need to set aside profit, but your bank account is commingled with operating expense, so nothing happens.
3. **You're aware of the gap but haven't automated the fix.** You know what to do, but manual transfers don't happen consistently.

The fix is: (1) Calculate exactly what you need per account. (2) Set up separate accounts to make allocation physical. (3) Automate the process so it happens every week or every time revenue hits the bank.
