## Workflow: 12 Profit Points Optimization

A four-phase system to activate 2-3 high-leverage profit dials each quarter, measure results, and rotate. Built for founder-led agencies (€500K-€5M revenue, 3-30 people) where precision beats diffusion.

---

## Mode Detection

**Consultant mode** → You're running the session WITH a client/founder. Guide them through discovery questions, help them score each point, debate priorities together, design actions collaboratively.

**Self-service mode** → The founder is scoring alone. Provide templates, examples, and guardrails. Push for data-backed scores (not guesses). Be the voice of precision and prioritization.

Both modes follow the same four phases. The difference is dialogue (consultant) vs. scaffolding (self-service).

---

## Phase 1: Scoring (2-3 hours, start of quarter)

**Goal:** Create baseline scorecard of 12 profit points with evidence-based 1-10 ratings.

### Step 1: Gather Evidence (30 min per point, 12 points)

For EACH profit point, identify:
- **Current metric:** What are we measuring? (e.g., average project fee, DSO days, close rate %)
- **Current value:** The actual number (e.g., €4,500, 50 days, 30%)
- **Benchmark:** How does it compare to industry or past performance?
- **Visible symptoms:** What does this show up as in the business? (e.g., win rate too high, scope creep, cash gaps)
- **Data source:** Bank statement? CRM? Calendar? Gut feel? (Confidence matters)

**Consultant mode:** Ask these questions one point at a time. Dig for real numbers. If they say "I think we're underpriced," ask "What's the actual average project fee? What's your win rate? What do competitors charge?" Push until you have grounding.

**Self-service mode:** Load the template from `references/profit-points-scoring-guide.md`. Fill one section at a time. Don't guess. If you don't have a number, find it in your CRM/P&L/calendar or note it as a data gap.

### Step 2: Score Each Point (1-10)

Use this framework:

| Score | Definition | Signal |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 1-2 | Crisis. Hemorrhaging money or killing growth. | Immediate action required. |
| 3-4 | Major gap. Clear improvement opportunity. | Prioritize for next quarter. |
| 5-6 | Decent but improvable. Room for upside. | Candidate if impact/effort favorable. |
| 7-8 | Good. Optimized. Low-hanging fruit exhausted. | Maintain. Only focus if compounding. |
| 9-10 | Excellent. Best-in-class. No improvement needed. | Don't touch. Invest time elsewhere. |

**Rule:** If you can't explain a score in 1-2 sentences backed by data, rescore with real metrics.

**Edge case: Business scoring 7+ on most points.** When a business is already well-optimized (most scores 7-10), shift strategy:
- **Focus on compounding:** Move 7→9 on key revenue drivers (Pricing, Lead Conversion, Lead Quality).
- **Look for interaction effects:** Pricing + Efficiency multiplies impact. Volume + Quality compounding. These are where mature businesses unlock the next growth wave.
- **Maintenance mode:** Quarterly scoring becomes a health check, not a transformation tool. Use it to catch any point that slips below 7.
- **Graduate to strategic growth:** If all 12 points are 7+, the constraint is no longer optimization — it's market opportunity, positioning, or delegation. Consider shifting to strategic growth planning, new service lines, or market expansion instead of tweaking dials.

### Step 3: Create Scorecard

Compile all 12 points into a table with:
- Point name
- Current score (1-10)
- 1-2 sentence explanation
- Data source (confidence check)
- Trend (↑ improving, ↓ declining, → stable)

**Output Format - Profit Points Scorecard:**
```
| # | Profit Point | Current Score | Evidence | Data Source | Trend | Why This Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Expenses | [1–10] | Monthly OpEx €[X] | Bank statement | ↑/↓/→ | [1–2 sentences] |
| 2 | Pricing | [1–10] | Avg project €[X]; Win rate [Y]% | Invoices | ↑/↓/→ | [1–2 sentences] |
[...continue for all 12...]
```

## Phase 2: Opportunity Analysis (2-3 hours, same day as scoring)

**Goal:** For each point scoring below 7, estimate what a 2-point improvement unlocks in €/year and effort hours.

### Step 1: Identify Below-7 Points

Pull all scores of 1-6 from your scorecard. These are candidates.

### Step 2: Calculate Impact & Effort

For each below-7 point, estimate:

- **Impact:** Revenue/margin benefit of moving +2 points (e.g., "10% price increase = €45K/year")
- **Effort:** Hours required to achieve improvement (e.g., "Value audit (10 hrs) + proposal redesign (40 hrs) + training (4 hrs) = 54 hrs")
- **Ratio:** Impact ÷ Effort (e.g., €45K ÷ 54 hrs = €833/hour)

**Reference:** Load `references/profit-points-scoring-guide.md` for impact/effort examples for each point.

### Step 3: Create Impact/Effort Matrix

**Output Format - Prioritization Matrix:**
```
| Profit Point | Current Score | 2-Point Impact (€/year) | Effort (hours) | Impact/Effort Ratio | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| #2 Pricing | 4 | €45K | 54 | €833/hr | A |
| #6 Lead Conversion | 6 | €120K | 30 | €4,000/hr | A |
| #12 Time to Cashflow | 4 | €50K-€100K | 15-20 | €2,500-€5,000/hr | A |
[...]
```

**Rule:** Anything >€1,000/hour is a focus candidate. <€300/hour gets deprioritized unless in crisis.

---

## Phase 3: Quarterly Focus Selection (1-2 hours, same session)

**Goal:** Select 2-3 highest-ratio points. Lock in measurable targets. Design 3-5 concrete actions per point.

### Step 1: Select Top 2-3 Points

Pick points with highest impact/effort ratios. **Bias toward:**
- High impact (€50K+/year)
- Low effort (<60 hours)
- Founder can execute (don't over-commit)
- Mix revenue and cost points (don't unbalance)

**CCC-typical selection:** Pricing + Lead Conversion + Time to Cashflow (classic trio for founder-led agencies).

### Step 2: Design 3-5 Concrete Actions Per Point

**An action is specific, assigned, deadline-driven.** NOT "improve pricing" — that's hope, not a plan.

**Output Format - Quarterly Focus Plan:**
```
QUARTERLY FOCUS POINT: #2 Pricing (Current Score: 4 → Target Score: 6)

Target: Increase average project fee from €4,500 to €5,200 (+15%). Maintain/improve 40% close rate.

Action 1: Value audit — interview 5 existing happy clients, quantify business results | Owner: [Name] | Deadline: [Date] | Success metric: 3+ concrete ROI stories documented

Action 2: Redesign proposal template — show three tiers (€5K, €5.5K, €6.5K) with anchoring | Owner: [Name] | Deadline: [Date] | Success metric: New template in all new proposals

Action 3: Sales training — walk through new value messaging + price objection handling | Owner: [Name] | Deadline: [Date] | Success metric: Team confident on new talking points

Action 4: Test pricing on 5 proposals — track close rate and objections daily | Owner: [Name] | Deadline: [Date] | Success metric: Close rate holds at 40%+

Action 5: Mid-quarter checkpoint — adjust pricing/messaging if needed | Owner: [Name] | Deadline: [Date] | Success metric: Decision on permanent increase

Expected quarterly impact: +€12K revenue (if 6 deals close at new rate)
Quarterly review date: [Last day of quarter]
```

## Complete Quarterly Cycle Example (Anonymized)

**This example shows what a full 90-day cycle looks like — from scoring through measurement to CBS impact.**

### Q2 Baseline Scores (Start of Quarter)
| Profit Point | Score | Evidence | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing | 4 | Avg project €4,500; close rate 42% | Underpriced vs. market; many low-fit inquiries |
| Lead Conversion | 6 | 42% win rate on qualified leads | Decent but inconsistent qualification |
| Time to Cashflow | 4 | DSO 50 days; €120K receivables outstanding | Slow payment terms; weak follow-up |
| Expenses | 7 | OpEx stable at 35% of revenue | Well-controlled, no crisis |
| [Other 8 points scored similarly...] | — | — | — |

### Impact/Effort Analysis
| Point | Score | €/Year Impact (2-pt move) | Hours | Ratio | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing | 4 | €45K (10% price ↑) | 54 | €833/hr | **A** |
| Lead Conversion | 6 | €75K (better qualification) | 40 | €1,875/hr | **A** |
| Time to Cashflow | 4 | €65K (DSO 50→42) | 20 | €3,250/hr | **A** |

**Selection rationale:** All three exceed €1K/hour. Pricing requires upfront investment but multiplies. Lead Conversion is owner-driven. Time to Cashflow releases working capital immediately.

### Q2 Action Plan (3 actions per point, anonymized)

**PRICING (Target: 4 → 5.5)**
- Action 1: Interview 5 existing happy clients, document ROI stories (3-5 sentences each) | Completed week 2 | Discovered clients realized €150K-€400K value
- Action 2: Redesign proposal with three-tier anchoring (€5K, €5.8K, €6.8K) | Completed week 3 | New template used in all proposals from week 4 onward
- Action 3: Hold 2-hour sales training on value messaging + price objection handling | Completed week 5 | Team confidence improved; two early deals closed at new tier 2 pricing

**LEAD CONVERSION (Target: 6 → 7)**
- Action 1: Document ideal client profile from 10 best past projects | Completed week 1 | Clear pattern: B2B SaaS, €500K+ revenue, own sales problem
- Action 2: Tighten intake form + first-call qualification scorecard | Completed week 4 | Screen 3 low-fit inquiries before discovery; saved 12 hours wasted time
- Action 3: Test 2-minute qualification call before full discovery | Ongoing week 6+ | Surfaced 5 bad-fit leads early; improved effective discovery-to-close ratio

**TIME TO CASHFLOW (Target: 4 → 5)**
- Action 1: Change payment terms from Net 30 to Net 15 + 2% early-pay discount | Completed week 1 | Incentive took 2 clients immediately
- Action 2: Set up automated payment reminders + dunning sequence (Stripe/email) | Completed week 2 | Reduced follow-up admin; caught 3 late invoices 5 days earlier
- Action 3: Weekly collections standup every Monday morning (5 min) | Ongoing | Tracked 8 invoices at risk; recovered €18K in week 8

### Mid-Quarter Checkpoint (Week 7)
- Pricing: 2 proposals sent at new tier 2; 1 accepted; 1 objection on price (low-fit anyway) — **on track**
- Lead Conversion: Screened 6 new inquiries; 4 qualified in; 2 rejected upfront — **ahead of target**
- Time to Cashflow: DSO down to 46 days (from 50); €12K accelerated — **exceeding target**

### Q2 End-of-Quarter Results

**PRICING: 4 → 5.5 (Target: +2 points)**
- Actual: 5.5 (13% price increase on 60% of new proposals; blended 5.5%)
- New avg project fee: €5,085 (up from €4,500; 13% increase)
- Close rate on new pricing: 41% (vs. 42% baseline; minimal impact)
- Revenue impact: +€22K in Q2 (6 deals at new pricing)
- **Result: 57% of target €45K impact delivered in one quarter** (rest compounds future quarters)

**LEAD CONVERSION: 6 → 6.8 (Target: +1 point)**
- Actual: 6.8 (qualification improved; fewer wasted discoverys)
- Close rate on qualified leads: 44% (up from 42%)
- Wasted discovery calls eliminated: 4 (12 hours saved)
- Revenue impact: €18K (2 additional deals closed from better qualification + faster sales cycle)
- **Result: +€18K from improved conversion + €12K time savings**

**TIME TO CASHFLOW: 4 → 5.0 (Target: +1 point)**
- DSO: 50 days → 42 days (8-day improvement)
- Working capital freed: €28K (calculated on monthly revenue €200K)
- Overdue invoices at quarter-end: 2 (vs. 6 in prior quarter)
- **Result: +€28K working capital + better cash predictability**

### CBS Impact Calculation

**Before Q2:**
- Profit%: 18% (€180K profit on €1M revenue)
- Owner%: 60% (spending 20 hours/week on client work + 8 on admin)
- Tax%: 22% (effective tax rate)
- **CBS total: 100 points**

**After Q2:**
- Profit%: 19.8% (18% + 1.8 from pricing + 0.5 from lead efficiency - 0.5 from sales training cost)
- Owner%: 62% (2-hour weekly time savings from automation + streamlined qualification = 8 hours/week freed for strategy)
- Tax%: 22% (unchanged)
- **CBS total: 103.8 points (+3.8 point improvement)**

### Key Learnings for Next Quarter

1. **Pricing holds:** Feared close rate drop didn't materialize; can move to tier 2 as default (confidence: high).
2. **Lead qualification is currency:** Filtering bad fit early compounded with pricing — better clients at higher price = win-win.
3. **DSO is quick leverage:** 8-day improvement cost almost nothing and freed €28K; prioritize this quarterly.
4. **Compounding ready:** Start Q3 by improving Lead Conversion from 6.8→8 (better sales process + nurture sequence).

## Phase 4: Execution & Tracking (30 min/week, ongoing)

**Goal:** Execute actions on schedule. Track weekly. Measure results at quarter-end.

### Step 1: Weekly Execution Standup (Every Friday, 15 min)

**Format:** Review 3-5 actions from selected points. Answer:

```
WEEK OF [DATE]

PROFIT POINT #2: PRICING
Action 1: Value audit | Status: ✓ DONE / ⏳ IN PROGRESS / ❌ BLOCKED | Notes:
Action 2: Proposal redesign | Status: ⏳ IN PROGRESS | Notes: 80% complete, finalizing tier language
Action 3: Sales training | Status: ⏳ IN PROGRESS | Notes: Scheduled for Tuesday
Action 4: Price test on 5 proposals | Status: ⏳ IN PROGRESS | Notes: 2 proposals sent, 1 accepted at new price
Action 5: Mid-quarter checkpoint | Status: PENDING | Notes: Will assess end of month

[Repeat for other focus points...]

BLOCKERS: [Anything stopping progress?]
NEXT WEEK: [What needs to happen?]
```

**Rule:** If an action hasn't moved in 2 weeks, remove it or escalate as blocker.

### Step 2: Mid-Quarter Checkpoint (Week 7 of 13)

**Questions:**
- Are actions on track?
- Seeing early results? (prices holding, proposals converting, leads flowing?)
- Need to pivot any actions?
- On track to hit quarterly targets?

**If momentum is strong:** Keep going.
**If weak:** Debug. Either action ineffective or execution slipping.

### Step 3: Quarterly Measurement (Last day of quarter, 2 hours)

**For each 2-3 focus points, measure:**

```
PROFIT POINT #2: PRICING

Target: Increase €4,500 → €5,200 (+15%)
Actual: €4,750 (new deals); €4,620 (blended with old contracts)
Result: +5.6% (57% toward target)

Close rate on new pricing: 38% (vs. 40% baseline) — acceptable
Revenue impact: +€8K in quarter (vs. €12K target)

Actions completed: 5/5 on time
Learnings:
  - Value messaging resonated in 3/5 cases
  - Two deals lost to price alone (low-fit clients anyway)
  - Sales team gained confidence
  - Proposal template change improved close rate stability

Next quarter: Increase to 18% price increase on all proposals (confidence high)
```

**Create Quarterly Review Report:**
- Actual vs. Target for each point
- Total €/% impact
- Key learnings
- What worked, what didn't
- Top 3 focus points for next quarter (based on updated scoring)

### Step 4: Calculate CBS (Cashflow Business Score) Impact

**After scoring all 12 points and completing quarterly measurement, estimate how the top 2-3 improvements impacted CBS:**

CBS = Profit% + Owner% + Tax%

For your selected focus points (e.g., Pricing, Lead Conversion, Time to Cashflow), measure:
- **Profit% improvement:** How many percentage points better is net profit margin after each point improvement?
- **Owner% improvement:** Does better cashflow or margin free up time for the owner? (Proxy: reduced admin hours = higher Owner score.)
- **Tax% improvement:** Any structural tax wins from improved revenue or efficiency?

**Example calculation (anonymized quarterly cycle):**
- Pricing (4→5.5 actual): +1.5% profit margin (€18K on €1.2M revenue) = +1.5% to CBS
- Lead Conversion (6→6.8): +1.2% margin improvement (better deal closure rate) = +1.2% to CBS
- Time to Cashflow (4→5): DSO improved 50→42 days, releasing €28K working capital; Owner% +0.8 (less cash stress)

**Total CBS impact: +3.5%** (or +1.75 points on a 50-point CBS scale)

This connects profit point actions directly to the overall health metric. Use it in your quarterly review to show how operational tweaks compound into business health.

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Trying to improve all 12 at once** → Diffusion. Pick 2-3. Obsess.
2. **Picking comfortable points over high-impact ones** → Lead Conversion is harder than Lead Generation but worth more. Pick impact.
3. **Setting targets without actions** → Targets are hopes. Actions are plans.
4. **Ignoring Time to Cashflow (#12)** → DSO improvement releases €50K+ working capital. Do not ignore for cash-tight businesses.
5. **Measuring wrong metrics** → "Improved pricing" (vague) vs. "Price increased 12%, close rate held at 40%" (measurable).
6. **No weekly accountability** → Plans die in execution. Friday standup = minimum viable accountability.
7. **Rotating focus monthly** → Takes 4-6 weeks for results. Give each point 12 weeks of focus before rotating.
8. **Forgetting points interact** → Pricing + Efficiency together multiplies impact. Map dependencies.

---

## Rules

1. **Every score must have data.** If you can't name the metric and current value, rescore with real numbers.
2. **Impact/Effort ratio guides selection.** Don't pick "feels important" — pick highest ratio (>€1K/hour).
3. **3-5 actions per point, no more.** More = diffusion. Fewer = incomplete plan.
4. **Actions are specific, assigned, deadline-driven.** "Improve X" is not an action. "Test price increase on 5 proposals by April 30" is.
5. **Weekly tracking is non-negotiable.** Friday standup prevents momentum loss. If you skip it, you lose accountability.
6. **Don't pivot before 4-6 weeks.** Early results are noisy. Give actions time to compound.
7. **Measure all 12 every quarter.** Trending shows which points are improving (maybe deprioritize next quarter) and which are slipping (escalate).
