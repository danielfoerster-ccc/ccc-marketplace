## The 12 Profit Points at a Glance

| # | Point | Description | Typical Leverage |
|---|-------|-------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Expenses** | Systematic cost reduction; ruthless classification of waste vs. essential. | €2K–€10K/month reduction in OpEx. Fastest impact: 3–8 weeks. |
| 2 | **Pricing** | Increase prices without losing customers through value-based pricing, anchoring, packaging. | €20K–€100K/year additional revenue per 5–10% price rise. |
| 3 | **Margin** | Improve product/service margins through vendor terms, delivery optimization, higher-tier services. | 5–15% margin improvement = €10K–€50K/year on €500K revenue. |
| 4 | **Efficiency** | Deliver same value faster/cheaper through process optimization, automation, elimination of waste. | 20–40% time savings on delivery = €50K–€200K/year capacity freed. |
| 5 | **Lead Generation** | Generate more qualified leads through new channels, content, partnerships, referrals. | 20–50% increase in pipeline = €30K–€150K/year if conversion held constant. |
| 6 | **Lead Conversion** | Convert higher % of leads to clients through better sales process, proposals, follow-up. | 5–10% improvement in close rate = €25K–€100K/year on 100-lead pipeline. |
| 7 | **Lead Follow-up** | Better nurturing of warm leads through automated sequences, cadence, re-engagement. | 15–25% of dead leads re-activated = €10K–€50K/year with minimal effort. |
| 8 | **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** | Lower cost-per-acquisition through channel optimization, referral rewards, content scaling. | Halving CAC (e.g., €500 → €250) on 20 customers = €5K/month recovered cash. |
| 9 | **Retention / LTV** | Keep customers longer, increase total lifetime spend through upsells, renewals, loyalty. | Extending customer life from 12 to 18 months = 50% LTV increase. |
| 10 | **Average Order Value (AOV)** | Increase per-transaction revenue through bundling, upsells, premium tiers, add-ons. | €200 → €250 AOV on 50 deals/year = €2,500 additional revenue. |
| 11 | **Marketing ROI** | Better return on marketing spend through attribution, channel efficiency, cutting low-ROI tactics. | Cutting bottom-30% lowest-ROI spend = 15–25% margin improvement on marketing budget. |
| 12 | **Time to Cashflow** | Faster invoice collection, optimized payment terms, reduced Days Sales Outstanding (DSO). | DSO: 45 days → 30 days = €30K–€100K immediate working capital release. |

## Detailed Point Definitions

### #1: Expenses

**What it measures:** Annual operational spending (OpEx) broken into Fat/Muscle/Bone.
- **Fat** = waste, redundancy, founder vanity (cancel without impact)
- **Muscle** = essential, high-ROI tools/team
- **Bone** = absolutely critical (payroll, rent, insurance)

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Spending uncontrolled. "We spend about €60K/month but no one knows on what." No budget, no review.
- **Score 3-4:** Spending tracked but not optimized. €5K-€20K/month in Fat. PRUN never run.
- **Score 5-6:** Reasonable spending discipline. PRUN audit incomplete. Some Fat remains.
- **Score 7-8:** Well-optimized. PRUN completed. Fat aggressively cut. Vendor terms negotiated.
- **Score 9-10:** Lean as possible. All Fat eliminated. Muscle and Bone only.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Current monthly OpEx: €[X]
- Breakdown: Payroll € / Tools € / Contractors € / Rent € / Other €
- PRUN status: Completed? In progress? Never done?
- Identified Fat candidates (if any)
- Vendor contract terms (annual or monthly? Room to negotiate?)

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Monthly OpEx
Current value: €50K/month
Visible symptoms: Too many SaaS subscriptions, some unused. Contractor bench overhead. No formal budget.
Data source: Bank statement last 12 months
Confidence level: 8/10 (real bank data)
SCORE: 4
WHY THIS SCORE: Spending is tracked but not audited. PRUN never run. Likely €5K-€10K/month in Fat (unused tools, idle contractors). No vendor rate negotiations attempted.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 4–5 (easy win; first PRUN usually finds €3K-€15K/month in cuts).

### #2: Pricing

**What it measures:** How much you charge per unit/project relative to value and market.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Significantly underpriced. Charging at cost or below. Win rate >60%. Clients never negotiate. Clear money left on table.
- **Score 3-4:** Below market by 20-40%. Win rate >40%. Positioning as "budget option" or not articulating value.
- **Score 5-6:** Close to market average but untested. No recent price testing. One-size-fits-all pricing model.
- **Score 7-8:** Market-aligned. Tiered pricing. Regular testing. Price objections handled well.
- **Score 9-10:** Premium positioning. Clear value anchor. Selective customer base. Price is rarely an objection.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Average project fee: €[X]
- Win rate on proposals: [Y]%
- Competitor/market average: €[Z] (for comparison)
- Visible symptoms: Are clients negotiating? Are margins good? Do you feel like you're leaving money on table?
- Pricing model: Single tier or multiple options?
- Time since last price test/increase: [X] months/years

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Average project fee + Win rate
Current value: €4,500 avg; 42% win rate
Benchmark: Industry average €6K-€8K for comparable agencies
Visible symptoms: Win rate is high (good for volume, bad signal for underpricing). Clients never push back on scope, only price. "Any discounts for volume?" is a common ask.
Data source: 12 months of invoices; CRM
Confidence level: 9/10 (invoice data is real)
SCORE: 4
WHY THIS SCORE: Significantly underpriced relative to market. Win rate too high signals we're leaving €1.5K-€2K per deal on table. No recent price testing. This is classic agency underpricing.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 4–5 (underpricing is nearly universal; usually highest-impact opportunity).

### #3: Margin

**What it measures:** Gross profit margin on delivered services. (Revenue - Direct Costs) / Revenue.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Margin <20%. Direct costs too high. Unprofitable delivery model.
- **Score 3-4:** Margin 20-30%. Vendor costs high, no negotiation leverage. Scope creep eats margin.
- **Score 5-6:** Margin 30-40%. Reasonable but improvable. Vendor terms negotiable. Premium service tier not tried.
- **Score 7-8:** Margin 40-50%. Well-optimized. Strong vendor relationships. Service tiers in place.
- **Score 9-10:** Margin >50%. Lean delivery. Automated components. Recurring revenue mix.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Current gross margin: [X]%
- Direct cost breakdown: Subcontractors € / Tools/software € / Freelance labor € / Other €
- Vendor contract terms: Month-to-month or committed? Any rate pressure?
- Scope creep estimate: How much work falls outside initial scope?

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Gross margin %
Current value: 38% (€500K revenue, ~€310K direct costs)
Benchmark: Agency average 35-45%
Visible symptoms: Some scope creep (5-10% of projects). Subcontractor rates rising. No premium service tier.
Data source: P&L last 12 months
Confidence level: 7/10 (P&L data solid, but cost allocation rough)
SCORE: 5
WHY THIS SCORE: Decent margin but room for improvement. Better subcontractor terms + premium tier could push to 42-45%. Scope creep alone costs 1-2 margin points.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 5–6.

### #4: Efficiency

**What it measures:** Time and resources required to deliver the same service. Delivery hours per project. Utilization rate. Scope creep %.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Highly inefficient. Projects take 2x expected time. Founder is severe bottleneck. Scope creep >20%.
- **Score 3-4:** Inefficient. Processes not documented. Founder involved in all delivery. Automation minimal. Scope creep 10-15%.
- **Score 5-6:** Reasonable. Some process documentation. Minor bottlenecks. Scope creep 5-10%. Automation opportunities exist.
- **Score 7-8:** Efficient. Processes mapped. Scope-managed. Founder not in critical path. Automation in place for 70%+ of work.
- **Score 9-10:** Highly efficient. Systematic delivery. Minimal scope creep. Founder unbottlenecked. Team operates independently.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Average delivery hours per project: [X]
- Estimated time if process optimized: [Y]
- % of time on scope creep (out-of-scope asks): [Z]%
- Bottlenecks: Is founder the limiting factor?
- Automation status: Which tasks are manual that could be automated?

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Avg delivery hours per project
Current value: 200 hours avg (range 150-250)
Benchmark: Process optimization could achieve 160-180 hours
Visible symptoms: Founder is in every project. Manual QA reviews. Scope creep common (scope says 200, actual is often 220+). Documentation incomplete.
Data source: Time tracking (rough)
Confidence level: 5/10 (time tracking inconsistent)
SCORE: 4
WHY THIS SCORE: Founder as bottleneck. Process waste visible. 15-20% time savings available via process mapping, automation, and founder unbottlenecking. This unlocks €50K-€80K capacity on €500K revenue.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 4–5 (founder bottleneck is universal; high-leverage if solved).

### #5: Lead Generation

**What it measures:** Volume and quality of inbound/outbound leads. Qualified leads per month.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Almost no leads. No systematic pipeline. "Waiting for referrals" or minimal outreach.
- **Score 3-4:** 2-4 leads/month. Mix of channels unclear. Low conversion suggests quality issues.
- **Score 5-6:** 5-8 leads/month. 1-2 channels working. Referral program nascent. Content underperforming.
- **Score 7-8:** 8-12 leads/month. 2-3 channels reliably generating. Referral program active. Content producing leads.
- **Score 9-10:** 12+ leads/month. Multiple channels working. Predictable pipeline. Referral system efficient.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Qualified leads per month (average last 12 months): [X]
- Lead sources: Referral % / Content % / Outbound % / Paid ads % / Other %
- Lead quality: Are leads typically low-fit, mid-fit, or high-fit? (Conversion rate is proxy)
- Which channels are working? Which underperforming?

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Qualified leads per month
Current value: 6 leads/month (range 3-10)
Breakdown: Referral 50%, Content 30%, Outbound cold 20%, Paid ads 0%
Visible symptoms: Referral program working (happy clients refer), but not systematic. Content strategy vague. Outbound sporadic.
Data source: CRM tracking (moderate confidence)
Confidence level: 6/10
SCORE: 6
WHY THIS SCORE: Decent pipeline volume but unevenly generated. Referral program (50%) is working but could be expanded. Content and outbound both have runway. No paid ads tested. This is an 8-12 leads/month business with right effort.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 5–6.

### #6: Lead Conversion

**What it measures:** % of leads that close to become clients. Close rate.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Close rate <15%. Sales process broken. Proposals weak. No follow-up.
- **Score 3-4:** Close rate 15-25%. Sales process exists but weak. Proposals generic. Objection handling poor.
- **Score 5-6:** Close rate 25-35%. Sales process solid. Proposals decent but not client-specific. Some objections not handled.
- **Score 7-8:** Close rate 35-45%. Sales process optimized. Proposals client-specific and outcome-focused. Objections pre-empted.
- **Score 9-10:** Close rate >45%. Sales process laser-focused. Proposals are consultative. Objections rare.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Current close rate (% of leads that become clients): [X]%
- Sales process: How many touchpoints? How long from first call to contract?
- Proposal quality: Generic template or client-specific? Do they include ROI estimates or just scope?
- Top objections: What causes deals to fall through?

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Close rate %
Current value: 30% (on ~6 leads/month = 1.8 closes/month)
Visible symptoms: Proposals are hand-written, generic. No clear next steps defined. Objection to "timeline" and "budget" common. No proposal template.
Data source: CRM
Confidence level: 8/10
SCORE: 5
WHY THIS SCORE: Below-average close rate. Sales process exists but inefficient. Proposal redesign alone could add 5-10% to close rate. Better objection handling could add another 3-5%. Realistic target: 40% close rate (5-point improvement).
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 5–6 (often the second-highest leverage point after Pricing).

### #7: Lead Follow-up

**What it measures:** % of "no" leads that convert after initial rejection. Nurturing effectiveness.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Almost no follow-up. "No" means lost. 0-2% of rejections convert later.
- **Score 3-4:** Sporadic follow-up. No system. Manual, inconsistent outreach. 2-5% convert.
- **Score 5-6:** Basic nurturing. Email sequences exist but not optimized. 5-10% convert.
- **Score 7-8:** Strong nurturing. Automated sequences. Regular touchpoints. 10-15% of "no" leads convert.
- **Score 9-10:** Systematic nurturing. Multi-channel sequences. Warm leads are re-engaged consistently. 15-25% convert.

**Evidence to gather:**
- % of "no" leads that eventually convert: [X]%
- Follow-up system: Email sequences? CRM automation? Manual outreach?
- Average time from "no" to eventual "yes": [X] months
- Abandoned warm leads (qualified but not yet closed): [X] leads in pipeline

**Example:**
```
Current metric: % of warm leads that convert after initial "no"
Current value: 5% (roughly 3 deals/year from warm re-engagement)
Visible symptoms: No systematic follow-up. Leads tagged "follow up in 3 months" but often forgotten. No automated sequences. Dead leads in CRM never revisited.
Data source: CRM observation
Confidence level: 4/10 (rough estimate)
SCORE: 3
WHY THIS SCORE: Major leak. With automated sequence (4-part email, 2-week cadence), 10-15% of warm leads would convert. On ~80 warm leads/year, that's 8-12 additional closures = €40K-€60K revenue. High impact, low effort.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 3–4 (usually neglected, high-impact).

### #8: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**What it measures:** Cost to acquire one customer. CAC = (Sales + Marketing spend) / (# of new customers).

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** CAC very high. CAC:LTV ratio <1:5. Unprofitable channel mix.
- **Score 3-4:** CAC high relative to LTV. CAC:LTV ratio 1:5 to 1:10. Expensive channels.
- **Score 5-6:** CAC reasonable. CAC:LTV ratio 1:10 to 1:20. Some channels more efficient than others.
- **Score 7-8:** CAC low. CAC:LTV ratio 1:20 to 1:30. Channel mix optimized.
- **Score 9-10:** CAC very low. CAC:LTV ratio >1:30. Referral-driven or highly optimized.

**Evidence to gather:**
- CAC (total cost to acquire one customer): €[X]
- LTV (lifetime value of one customer): €[Y]
- CAC:LTV ratio: 1:[Z]
- Breakdown by channel: Paid ads CAC / Referral CAC / Content CAC
- Which channel has lowest CAC?

**Example:**
```
Current metric: CAC + CAC:LTV ratio
Current value: CAC €600; LTV €18K; Ratio 1:30 (positive)
Breakdown: Paid ads €800 CAC, Referral €300 CAC, Content €700 CAC
Visible symptoms: Paid ads channel expensive. Referral program dormant. Content CAC unclear.
Data source: Marketing platform + CRM
Confidence level: 6/10 (attribution fuzzy)
SCORE: 5
WHY THIS SCORE: CAC is acceptable (positive CAC:LTV ratio), but expensive. Referral rewards program could reduce CAC to €400. On 20 customers/year, that's €4K-€6K annual savings. Reallocation from paid ads to referral could improve further.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 5–6.

### #9: Customer Retention & LTV

**What it measures:** How long customers stay. Customer lifetime value (LTV) = (Avg customer lifetime in months) × (Avg annual spend ÷ 12).

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** High churn. Avg lifetime <6 months. Churn rate >30%. LTV very low.
- **Score 3-4:** Moderate churn. Avg lifetime 6-9 months. Churn rate 20-30%. Low LTV.
- **Score 5-6:** Acceptable retention. Avg lifetime 12-15 months. Churn rate 10-20%. Upsell opportunity.
- **Score 7-8:** Good retention. Avg lifetime 18-24 months. Churn rate <10%. Upsells in place.
- **Score 9-10:** Excellent retention. Avg lifetime >24 months. Churn rate <5%. Upsells + renewals systematic.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Average customer lifetime (months): [X]
- Total lifetime spend per customer (avg): €[Y]
- Churn rate (% of customers lost per year): [Z]%
- Repeat customer %: [A]%
- Upsell/renewal rate: [B]%

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Avg customer lifetime + total spend
Current value: 12 months avg lifetime; €5K total spend per customer; 60% churn rate
Visible symptoms: Customers rarely renew. Onboarding weak (customers don't see ROI early). Upsell not attempted. Contracts are one-off, not renewed.
Data source: CRM customer history
Confidence level: 7/10
SCORE: 5
WHY THIS SCORE: Retention is below industry average. Extending lifetime from 12 to 18 months = 50% LTV increase. On 20 customers/year, that's €15K additional lifetime value (if each customer spend increases from €5K to €7.5K via upsell + renewal). Opportunity is high but requires onboarding/success process redesign.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 5–6.

### #10: Average Order Value (AOV)

**What it measures:** Average revenue per transaction/project. AOV = Annual revenue / # of deals closed.

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Very low AOV. €2K-€3K per deal. No bundling or upsells. "Budget option" positioning.
- **Score 3-4:** Low AOV. €3K-€4K per deal. Single tier only. Upsells rare.
- **Score 5-6:** Moderate AOV. €4K-€6K per deal. Some bundling. Upsells possible but not systematic.
- **Score 7-8:** Healthy AOV. €6K-€8K per deal. Multiple tiers. Upsells in 30-50% of deals.
- **Score 9-10:** High AOV. €8K+ per deal. Premium tier strong. Upsells in >50% of deals.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Average project value: €[X]
- Price tiers (if any): €[Y], €[Z], €[A] (Good/Better/Best)
- % of upsells (deals where customer buys more than initially quoted): [B]%
- Range of deal sizes: [Min] - [Max]

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Average project value
Current value: €4,500 per deal
Visible symptoms: No tiered pricing. All deals roughly same size (€4K-€5K). No upsell model. Customers don't know about premium options.
Data source: Invoices last 12 months
Confidence level: 8/10
SCORE: 6
WHY THIS SCORE: Moderate but improvable. Introducing three tiers (€3.5K, €5K, €7K) and educating on premium option could push AOV to €5.5K-€6K. On 40 deals/year, that's €20K-€40K additional revenue with no extra cost.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 6–7.

### #11: Marketing ROI

**What it measures:** Return on marketing spend. MROI = (Attributed revenue from marketing) / (Total marketing spend).

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** Negative or near-zero ROI. Marketing spend >20% of revenue. Unattributed spend. Wasteful.
- **Score 3-4:** Poor ROI. MROI 1:2 (€2 revenue per €1 spent). Most channels underperforming.
- **Score 5-6:** Acceptable ROI. MROI 1:4 (€4 per €1). Some channels strong, others weak.
- **Score 7-8:** Good ROI. MROI 1:5-1:6 (€5-€6 per €1). Most channels profitable.
- **Score 9-10:** Excellent ROI. MROI 1:8+ (€8+ per €1). Highly optimized channel mix.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Total annual marketing spend: €[X]
- Attributed revenue from marketing: €[Y]
- MROI: [Z]:1 (€[Z] revenue per €1 spent)
- Breakdown by channel: Paid ads MROI / Content MROI / Social MROI / Email MROI
- Which channels are bottom performers?

**Example:**
```
Current metric: Marketing ROI (attributed revenue / spend)
Current value: €50K/year marketing spend; €200K attributed revenue; MROI 1:4
Breakdown: Paid ads 1:2.5 (poor), Content 1:5 (good), Email 1:6 (excellent), Social 1:1.5 (terrible)
Visible symptoms: Social media ads underperforming but still funded. Content strategy strong but undersupported. Email sequences working but not leveraged.
Data source: Marketing platform + CRM attribution (moderate)
Confidence level: 6/10 (attribution rough)
SCORE: 5
WHY THIS SCORE: Acceptable but improvable. Cutting bottom 30% of spend (€15K from social/paid underperformers) and reallocating to content/email could improve MROI to 1:5. At same spend level, that's €10K additional profit margin.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 5–6.

### #12: Time to Cashflow (Days Sales Outstanding / DSO)

**What it measures:** Average days from invoice to payment received. DSO = (Average Accounts Receivable) / (Daily Revenue).

**How to score:**
- **Score 1-2:** DSO >60 days. Payment terms net-60+. Poor collections process. Invoices often forgotten.
- **Score 3-4:** DSO 45-60 days. Net-30 terms but payment delayed. Collection follow-up inconsistent.
- **Score 5-6:** DSO 30-45 days. Net-30 terms, roughly honored. Room for improvement in deposits/collections.
- **Score 7-8:** DSO 20-30 days. Mostly upfront deposits or fast collection. Systematic collections.
- **Score 9-10:** DSO <20 days. 50%+ upfront. Near-zero receivables. Cash collected before delivery.

**Evidence to gather:**
- Current DSO (days to payment): [X] days
- Payment terms offered: Net-[Y] (e.g., Net-30)
- Actual payment timing (median days received): [Z] days
- Deposits collected upfront: [A]%
- Invoicing process: When do you invoice? (Day 1 of work or at project end?)

**Example:**
```
Current metric: DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)
Current value: 50 days average (range 30-70)
Payment terms: Net-30 offered, but often paid Net-45 or later
Upfront deposits: 0% (no deposits collected)
Visible symptoms: Invoices sent at project end, not start. Delayed invoicing causes further delays. No collections follow-up for overdue invoices.
Data source: A/R aging report
Confidence level: 9/10 (bank data)
SCORE: 3
WHY THIS SCORE: Poor cashflow. DSO 50 days locks up €50K in receivables at any time (€500K revenue ÷ 365 days × 50 days). Moving to net-30 + 30% upfront deposits could reduce to 25-30 days, releasing €30K-€40K working capital immediately. This is a cash emergency for most agencies — high priority.
```

**Typical baseline for founder-led agencies:** 3–4 (usually critical; second-highest-leverage point after Pricing).

## Scoring Framework

| Score | Definition | Situation | Action Signal |
|-------|-----------|-----------|---|
| 1–2 | **Crisis** | Hemorrhaging money or killing growth. Immediate intervention required. | **Immediate focus required.** This point threatens business viability. |
| 3–4 | **Major Gap** | Clear improvement opportunity. 20-40% upside available. Low hanging fruit exists. | **Prioritize for next quarter.** Include in 2-3 focus points. |
| 5–6 | **Decent but Improvable** | Room for upside but not urgent. 10-20% improvement available. | **Candidate for focus if impact/effort favorable.** Lower priority than 3-4. |
| 7–8 | **Good** | Optimized. Low-hanging fruit exhausted. Incremental gains possible. | **Maintain, don't over-invest.** Only focus if compounding with other points. |
| 9–10 | **Excellent** | Best-in-class. No improvement needed. | **Don't touch.** Allocate resources to 3-4 and 5-6 points instead. |

**Rule:** If you can't explain a score in 1-2 sentences backed by data, it's a guess. Rescore.

## Impact/Effort Examples

For each profit point, here's how to estimate the impact (€ or % improvement) and effort (hours) of moving +2 points.

### #1 Expenses: 2-Point Improvement Example

**Current scenario:**
- Score: 4 (spending €50K/month unaudited)
- 2-point target: Score 6 (lean spending with formal audit)

**Impact calculation:**
- PRUN audit typically uncovers 10-20% of OpEx as Fat
- €50K × 15% = €7.5K/month = €90K/year savings
- **Impact: €7.5K/month or €90K/year cost reduction**

**Effort calculation:**
- PRUN audit: 40 hours
- Implementation (renegotiate, cancel): 20 hours
- Training/transition: 5 hours
- **Effort: 65 hours (over 8-12 weeks)**

**Impact/Effort ratio:** €90K ÷ 65 hrs = €1,385/hour ✓ (High priority)

---

### #2 Pricing: 2-Point Improvement Example

**Current scenario:**

## High-Leverage Action Tactics

For each profit point, here are 2-3 proven tactics that typically work:

### #1 Expenses — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: PRUN Audit**
- Classify all expenses as Fat/Muscle/Bone
- Cut Fat ruthlessly (unused subscriptions, redundant tools, founder vanity spend)
- Typical result: €3K-€15K/month savings (8-16 weeks)

**Tactic 2: Vendor Renegotiation**
- Call existing vendors (software, services, contractors) and negotiate rates
- Most have 10-30% room for negotiation if you have alternatives
- Typical result: €500-€3K/month savings (4 weeks per vendor)

**Tactic 3: Subscription Audit**
- List all recurring charges (SaaS, subscriptions, memberships)
- Cancel unused or redundant ones
- Typical result: €200-€500/month (1 week effort)

---

### #2 Pricing — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Value Audit + Anchoring**
- Interview 5 happy clients; document quantified business results they achieved
- Create value anchor in proposal ("this ROI justifies €X investment")
- Raise prices 10-15% using anchoring
- Typical result: €2K-€5K/month revenue increase, 40%+ win rate maintained

**Tactic 2: Tiered Pricing / Packaging**
- Offer three tiers: Starter (€3K), Standard (€5K), Premium (€8K)
- Educate on value of premium option
- Typical result: 15% average AOV increase, same or higher close rate

**Tactic 3: Price Testing**
- Hold current price on 50% of new proposals; test +10% on other 50%
- Measure close rate on each group
- Scale if close rate holds (usually does, within 2-3 points)
- Typical result: 8-12% sustainable price increase over 8-12 weeks

---

### #3 Margin — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Subcontractor Rate Renegotiation**
- Most subcontractors will take 10-20% less if guaranteed pipeline
- Typical result: €2K-€5K/month margin improvement

**Tactic 2: Streamline Delivery Process**
- Eliminate redundant QA, simplify approvals, reduce scope creep
- Map current workflow, identify 3-5 waste points, remove them
- Typical result: 5-10% margin improvement (same revenue, lower costs)

**Tactic 3: Premium Service Tier**
- Offer premium version (VIP support, faster turnaround, extra features) at 1.5-2x base price
- Market to top 20% of prospects
- Typical result: 5-10% of clients upgrade; €1K-€3K/month margin lift

---

### #4 Efficiency — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Process Documentation + Optimization**
- Map current delivery workflow (start to finish)
- Identify bottlenecks, waste, founder dependencies
- Remove/automate 2-3 biggest waste points
- Typical result: 15-25% time savings

**Tactic 2: Automation**
- Identify repetitive tasks (proposal generation, reporting, QA, invoicing)
- Automate 2-3 via tools, scripts, or templates
- Typical result: 20-40 hours/month freed

**Tactic 3: Founder Unbottlenecking**
- Identify tasks founder does that others could do (QA, client calls, delivery)
- Hire or delegate 3-5 tasks
- Typical result: 20-30 hours/week founder capacity freed

---

### #5 Lead Generation — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Referral Program**
- Offer €500-€1K per referred client that closes
- Activate existing happy clients
- Typical result: +2-3 quality leads/month

**Tactic 2: Content Marketing**
- Create 1 cornerstone piece per quarter (guide, case study, webinar, blueprint)
- Target buyer's top pain point
- Typical result: +1-2 leads/month (takes 3-4 months to see results)

**Tactic 3: Strategic Partnerships**
- Identify 2-3 complementary businesses (not competitors)
- Establish mutual referral relationship
- Typical result: +1-2 warm leads/month

---

### #6 Lead Conversion — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Sales Process Audit**
- Map current journey from first call to signed contract
- Identify friction: missing collateral, slow responses, unclear next steps
- Fix 3-5 biggest friction points
- Typical result: +5-10% close rate

**Tactic 2: Proposal Redesign**
- Replace generic templates with client-specific, outcome-focused proposals
- Include client's business context, expected ROI, success metrics
- Typical result: +10-15% close rate

**Tactic 3: Objection Handling Training**
- Role-play top 5 sales objections with team
- Create response playbook for each
- Practice weekly
- Typical result: +3-7% close rate

---

### #7 Lead Follow-up — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Automated Email Sequence**
- Design 4-part "if client says no" email sequence
- Send every 2 weeks for 8 weeks
- Include new case study, updated offer, or re-engagement hook
- Typical result: 10-20% of "no" leads convert

**Tactic 2: Re-engagement Campaign**
- Pull all leads from past 12 months who said "not now"
- Send targeted message (new case study, updated pricing, limited-time offer)
- Typical result: 5-15% re-engagement

**Tactic 3: Calendar Block Follow-up**
- Block 2 hours/week for follow-up calls to warm leads
- Assign to one person or founder
- Track attempts and outcomes
- Typical result: 8-12% additional conversions

---

### #8 CAC — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Channel Mix Audit**
- Calculate CAC for each lead source
- Identify cheapest channel
- Reallocate spend to cheapest channels
- Typical result: 20-30% CAC reduction

**Tactic 2: Referral Rewards Program**
- Pay €500-€1K per referral that closes
- Eliminate or reduce paid advertising for that channel
- Typical result: CAC drops from €800 to €400

**Tactic 3: Sales Team Productivity**
- Measure CAC per salesperson
- Reward lower-CAC closers; coach high-CAC closers
- Typical result: 15-25% CAC reduction

---

### #9 Retention / LTV — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Renewal Process**
- 90 days before contract end, proactively schedule renewal conversation
- Make renewal easy (same-day decision option)
- Typical result: 10-20% churn reduction (= 10-20% LTV increase)

**Tactic 2: Upsell Playbook**
- Identify customers using <50% of services
- Create targeted upsell offer (more hours, premium tier, add-on)
- Pitch at 30, 60, 90-day milestone
- Typical result: €1K-€3K average upsell per customer

**Tactic 3: Customer Success Onboarding**
- Assign an owner to each new customer
- Schedule monthly check-ins (30 min, focused on customer's ROI)
- Typical result: 20-30% churn reduction

---

### #10 AOV — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Service Packaging**
- Redesign offering as three tiers: Starter (€3K), Standard (€5K), Premium (€8K)
- Educate on value difference between tiers
- Typical result: +10-15% average AOV

**Tactic 2: Add-on Bundling**
- Identify popular add-ons (rush service, extra revisions, analytics, extended support)
- Bundle at 20% discount to base service
- Typical result: +€500-€1K per deal

**Tactic 3: Proposal Psychology**
- When presenting three tiers, lead with middle tier as "recommended"
- Position premium as "for maximum ROI"
- Typical result: +15-25% AOV (psychological anchoring)

---

### #11 Marketing ROI — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Channel Audit + Cutoff**
- Rank all marketing channels by ROI (attributed revenue ÷ spend)
- Cut bottom 30% ruthlessly
- Reallocate to top 30%
- Typical result: 15-25% margin improvement on marketing budget

**Tactic 2: Attribution Cleanup**
- Tag all leads/customers with source (referral? content? paid ads? cold call?)
- Implement simple CRM tagging system
- Measure true channel ROI
- Typical result: +10-20% ROI improvement (just from visibility)

**Tactic 3: Paid Ads Optimization**
- Audit Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Facebook Ads
- Cut low-performing campaigns
- Increase bids on high-ROI campaigns
- Typical result: 20-30% CAC reduction on paid spend

---

### #12 Time to Cashflow (DSO) — 3 Tactics

**Tactic 1: Upfront Deposits**
- Require 30-50% deposit before work starts
- Reduces DSO significantly
- Typical result: DSO reduced by 15-25 days

**Tactic 2: Faster Invoicing**
- Invoice on day 1 of work (not at project end)
- Automated invoice generation and delivery
- Typical result: DSO reduced by 5-10 days

**Tactic 3: Collections Follow-up**
- For invoices >30 days overdue, send automated reminder email, then personal call
- Weekly A/R aging review
- Typical result: DSO reduced by 10-15 days
