# Audit Report — 5-Page Structure & Templates

This reference contains the exact templates for each page of the audit report. Fill in bracketed sections with client-specific data.

---

## Page 1: Executive Summary

```
## Executive Summary

[Your team is spending X hours per week on [PROCESS CATEGORY] that can be automated, costing approximately $X annually.]

### Key Opportunities

- **[Process Name]:** [Problem] → Save [X] hours/year ($[X])
- **[Process Name]:** [Problem] → Save [X] hours/year ($[X])
- **[Process Name]:** [Problem] → Save [X] hours/year ($[X])

### Total Opportunity

**First Year:** [X] hours saved | $[X] cost reduction
**Three Years:** [X] hours saved | $[X] cost reduction

### Recommended Next Step

[Implement [specific automation] to [specific outcome] within [timeframe].]
```

**Writing guidance:**
- The opening sentence is the headline — make it hit. Use the total weekly hours and annual cost.
- Key opportunities should be in VALUE score order (highest first).
- Each bullet follows the pattern: Process → Problem → Savings.
- The recommended next step is always the highest-scoring process, presented as a pilot.

## Page 2: Current State Assessment

```
## Current State Assessment

[2-3 sentences: industry, team size, what triggered the audit, key challenge]

### Evaluated Processes

| Process | Time/Exec | Weekly Volume | Monthly Hours | Pain |
|---------|-----------|---------------|---------------|------|
| [Name] | [X min] | [Y times] | [Z hrs] | 🔴 High |
| [Name] | [X min] | [Y times] | [Z hrs] | 🟡 Medium |
| [Name] | [X min] | [Y times] | [Z hrs] | 🟢 Low |

**Pain Rating:** 🔴 High (errors, client impact, complaints) | 🟡 Medium (inefficient but manageable) | 🟢 Low (acceptable)

### Current Tech Stack

- **CRM:** [Tool]
- **Project Management:** [Tool]
- **Communication:** [Tools]
- **File Storage:** [Tool]
- **[Category]:** [Tool]

### Integration Gaps

- [Tool A] → [Tool B]: [What's manual between them]
- [Tool C] → [Tool D]: [What's manual between them]

### Current vs. Proposed: [Top Process Name]

**Current Flow**
[Trigger]
→ [MANUAL] [Step 1]
→ [MANUAL] [Step 2]
→ [MANUAL] [Step 3]
...
Total: [X] min/execution | Error rate: ~[X]%

**Proposed Flow**
[Trigger]
→ [AUTO] [Step 1 automated]
→ [AUTO] [Step 2 automated]
→ [HUMAN] [Step requiring judgment]
...
Total: [X] min/execution (human review only) | Error rate: <[X]%

> "[Direct quote capturing the pain]"
> — **[Name]**, [Title]
```

**Writing guidance:**
- The process table should be sorted by monthly hours (highest first).
- Pain ratings come from the process docs — 🔴 means errors and customer impact, 🟡 means time waste, 🟢 means acceptable.
- The Before/After comparison is for the #1 VALUE-scored process only. Keep it visual — the contrast should be immediately obvious.
- The stakeholder quote is the emotional anchor. Pick the one that most viscerally captures the frustration.

## Page 3: Opportunity Matrix

```
## Opportunity Scoring (VALUE Framework)

| Process | V | A | L | U | E | Total | Priority |
|---------|---|---|---|---|---|-------|----------|
| [Name] | X | X | X | X | X | **XX** | 🔴 High |
| [Name] | X | X | X | X | X | **XX** | 🟡 Medium |
| [Name] | X | X | X | X | X | **XX** | 🟢 Low |

**V**=Volume | **A**=Automatable% | **L**=Labor hours | **U**=Users affected | **E**=Ease (inverse)
**Score:** 20-25: 🔴 High | 15-19: 🟡 Medium | 10-14: 🟢 Low | Below 10: Not recommended

### Implementation Order

**Phase 1 — Quick Wins (30-60 days)**
⭐ **[Process]** — Score: [XX] — [Why it's first: highest ROI, easiest win, proves value]

**Phase 2 — Foundation (60-120 days)**
**[Process]** — Score: [XX] — [Key benefit and why it's sequenced here]
**[Process]** — Score: [XX] — [Key benefit]

**Phase 3 — Expansion (120+ days)**
**[Process]** — Score: [XX] — [Key benefit, or note if deferred]
```

**Writing guidance:**
- Sort the table by total score, descending.
- The implementation order may differ from pure score ranking — explain why (e.g., "scores 19 but requires compliance review first, so sequenced after Quick Wins").
- Star (⭐) the recommended pilot — the single process to start with.

## Page 4: Solutions

```
## Solution Recommendations

### 1. [PROCESS NAME]

**Problem:** [Current pain in 1 sentence — from process doc]
**Solution:** [Tool] workflow: [Trigger] → [Auto Step 1] → [Auto Step 2] → [Output]
**Expected Result:** [Current time] → [Automated time] | Save [X] hrs/month ($[X]) | [Error improvement]

**Complexity:** [🟢 Low / 🟡 Medium / 🔴 High] | **Tool:** [Name] ($[X]/month) | **Setup:** [X] hours

---

### 2. [PROCESS NAME]
[Same format]

---

### 3. [PROCESS NAME]
[Same format]
```

**Complexity guide:**
- 🟢 Low = Pre-built templates available, <5 hours setup
- 🟡 Medium = Custom logic required, 5-9 hours setup
- 🔴 High = Complex integrations, 10+ hours setup

**Writing guidance:**
- Include 3-5 solutions, matching the top VALUE-scored processes.
- Every solution must name the exact tool(s) — "Make" not "an automation tool."
- The Solution line should read like a technical spec in plain English: trigger → steps → result.
- Monthly tool costs and setup hours must be specific, not ranges.

```
Week 13-14: [Action]

**Total Transformation Timeline:** [X] weeks
**Monthly hours saved at completion:** [X] hrs/month

```

**Writing guidance:**
- Each week should have a specific, tangible action — not "continue implementation."
- Milestones must be measurable: hours saved, error rates, deliverables shipped.
- The total summary at the bottom is the bridge to the proposal — it creates the natural "what does this cost?" question.
- Do NOT include pricing on this page. The proposal is a separate document.

---

## Formatting Notes

- Use tables for scores, process data, and comparisons — they scan faster than paragraphs.
- Color-code priority: 🔴 High | 🟡 Medium | 🟢 Low — consistent throughout.
- Keep language simple: "automate the email sequence" not "implement SMTP relay orchestration."
- Bold key numbers so they pop during a scan: **32 hours/month**, **$19,200/year**.
- The report will typically be delivered as a markdown file and may be converted to PDF by the consultant for client delivery.
