# Discovery Question Bank — by Type

*Reference doc for the [[ccc-sales-process]] plugin. Source: [[Gap Selling — Keenan]] Ch. 8. Recipe: [[Gap-Selling Discovery (Keenan)]].*

## The four question types

| Type | Function | Form |
|------|----------|------|
| **Probing** | Open-ended, press for specific details | "Tell me about… / Walk me through… / Help me understand…" |
| **Process** | Surface operational workflow ("how?") | "How do you currently…? What's your process for…?" |
| **Provoking** | Challenge assumptions, surface root cause | "What happens when…? Has there ever been a time when…? If you did X, what do you think would happen?" |
| **Validating** | Confirm shared understanding (NOT open-ended) | "What I hear you saying is… / Did I get this right? / Am I understanding correctly?" |

## Calvert's command-statement starter words

Use these instead of question-form interrogation. They invite deeper, less defensive answers.

- *Tell me a little bit about…*
- *Help me understand…*
- *Walk me through…*
- *Please describe…*
- *Share with me…*

## Question bank by discovery step

### Step 1 — Discover the Facts (Probing + Process)

Generic prompts (adapt to operator's industry):

- *"Walk me through how the [function] currently works at [company]."*
- *"Tell me about your team — structure, headcount, how decisions get made."*
- *"Help me understand what [tool / system / process] looks like today."*
- *"How many [units] do you handle per [time]? What's the trend over the last [period]?"*

Banish open-ended answers: when the buyer says *"we're not growing fast enough"*, press: *"Define that for me. What does fast enough mean to you?"* Numbers, not adjectives.

### Step 2 — Discover the Problems (Probing + Process)

- *"Where does the current process break down?"*
- *"What's frustrating about how this works today?"*
- *"Tell me about the last time [problem hypothesis from PIC] cost you something."*
- *"If you could wave a magic wand at one thing in this operation, what would it be?"*

Distinguish technical from business: *"Is the issue the system itself, or what the system is costing the business?"*

### Step 3 — Discover the Impact (Probing + Provoking)

- *"Tell me how this issue is affecting you."*
- *"Describe the impact it's having on your department."*
- *"What are the consequences every time this problem occurs?"*
- *"What does this cost you in [revenue / hours / headcount / customer churn / time-to-market]?"*

Force quantification. Open-ended answers are forbidden. *"Lost revenue"* is not an answer. *"€500K last year"* is.

### Step 4 — Discover the Root Cause (Provoking)

- *"Why do you think this problem is happening?"*
- *"How do you think your current processes are affecting this?"*
- *"What's underneath this — what's the real cause?"*
- *"Has this always been a problem, or did something shift?"*
- *"If you removed [obvious blocker], would the problem actually go away?"*

This step is where credibility is earned. The seller who articulates the root cause in industry terms earns peer-level standing.

### Step 5 — Discover the Future State (3 layers)

**Layer 1 — Technical**:
- *"How would you want this system to work?"*
- *"What would 'fixed' look like operationally?"*
- *"Walk me through the workflow you'd want."*

**Layer 2 — Business**:
- *"How much more revenue / time / margin / capacity would that produce?"*
- *"What would the impact be on [team / customer / unit economics]?"*
- *"Quantify it for me — in your numbers."*

**Layer 3 — Intrinsic Motivation**:
- After summarising back what they've said: *"Tell me a bit about what's driving this change."*
- Then: SHUT UP. Silence is the technique.

### Step 6 — Validating (throughout, not at end)

- *"What I hear you saying is [restate]. Did I get this right?"*
- *"So the priority order is [1, 2, 3]. Is that the right ranking?"*
- *"Let me make sure — the gap is [Future − Current = X]. Does that match what you see?"*

Use validating questions before transitioning to a new topic. They lock in shared understanding and prevent misdiagnosis.

## CCC-specific discovery questions (for the CCC ICP)

Pre-loaded for [[ccc-sales-prep]] when the prospect fits the [[ICP]] psychographic. These map to the [[CCC-PIC]]:

### For PIC 1.1-1.7 (AI / operational architecture)

- *"Walk me through your AI deployment journey so far. What works? What stalled?"*
- *"Where are you the bottleneck — what depends on you that shouldn't?"*
- *"If I asked your team 'who owns the AI strategy' — what would they say?"*
- *"Tell me about the consultants / partnerships you've tried. What worked, what didn't, and why?"*
- *"What does the comprehensive AI-native operation look like in your head, when you imagine it 18 months out?"* (Layer 1 future state)
- *"And if you got there — what would change for you personally?"* (Layer 3 intrinsic motivation)

### For PIC 2.1-2.3 (Cognitive / OS layer)

- *"Where does the team's shared context live today? Slack, email, docs, or in your head?"*
- *"When you're offline for a week, what stops working?"*
- *"Tell me about a recent decision you had to re-litigate because the team didn't have it written down."*

### For PIC 3.1-3.4 (Sales / revenue ops)

- *"Who can run a discovery call besides you?"*
- *"What does your last 5 stalled deals tell you about your sales process?"*
- *"How often do proposals get pushed back on price? What's the typical discount you end up giving?"*

### For PIC 5.1-5.2 (Time / capacity)

- *"In a typical week, what % of your calendar is on Invest / Produce work vs Delegate / Replace?"*
- *"What's the highest-leverage thing you should be doing that you currently can't get to?"*

## Anti-patterns

- **Pitch-disguised questions**: *"Wouldn't you agree that AI is critical?"* — that's a pitch with a question mark. Avoid.
- **Multi-question stacks**: *"How do you handle X, and what about Y, and what's your approach to Z?"* — pick one.
- **Closed questions where open ones work better**: *"Is this a problem?"* (yes/no) → *"How is this showing up in the operation?"* (open).
- **Sequence rigidity**: the question-types are not strictly linear. The ordering above is the diagnostic skeleton; conversational flow can move back and forth. Just make sure all four steps happen.
