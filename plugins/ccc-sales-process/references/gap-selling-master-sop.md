# Gap Selling — Master SOP

*Reference doc for the [[ccc-sales-process]] plugin. Distilled from [[Gap Selling — Keenan]] (2018). Lazy-loaded by skills when they need the full sequence.*

---

## What it is

A problem-centric selling methodology that replaces product-pitching with a rigorous excavation of the buyer's current state, future state, and the gap between them. The gap (Future − Current) is the value of the deal.

## When to run it

Any consultative B2B sale where the buyer has a quantifiable problem and your offering can produce a quantifiable outcome. **Mandatory** for CCC consulting engagements ≥ €5K. Optional but recommended for smaller deals.

## Prerequisites

- Problem Identification Chart (PIC): see [[CCC-PIC]] in CCC Foundations
- Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): derived from the PIC, ranked A/B/C/D by problem-count × impact-severity — see [[ICP]]
- A way to capture discovery notes that pass the CRM Challenge — in CCC, the person file at `03 - OPERATIONS/Relationships & Network/People/[Name].md`

## The full sequence

1. **Pre-Call Prep** — load PIC, hypothesise prospect's likely Current State, stage Probing/Process/Provoking/Validating questions for the 4 discovery steps (see `discovery-question-bank.md`)
2. **Outreach** — lead with a specific suspected problem from the PIC, not a pitch
3. **Discovery** (different session from demo, ideally different days)
   1. **Facts** (Probing + Process questions) — banish open-ended answers
   2. **Problems** — distinguish technical vs business
   3. **Impact** — quantify in buyer's currency
   4. **Root Cause** (Provoking questions)
   5. **Future State** — quantified deltas across 3 layers (Technical / Business / Intrinsic Motivation)
   6. **Intrinsic Motivation** — *"Tell me a bit about what's driving this change."* Then shut up.
4. **Gap Math** — Future − Current, expressed in client's units
5. **Qualification** — the 4 yes/no questions:
   1. Does the prospect have a problem you can fix?
   2. Does the prospect agree they have a problem?
   3. Does the prospect want to fix the problem?
   4. Will the prospect go on a journey with you to fix it?
6. **Demo** (if applicable) — 6 features max, anchored to discovered problems, no "ifs"
7. **Pipeline Movement** — Decision Criteria + Buying Process + Next Yes
8. **Troubleshooting** — *"I'm confused. You said…"* at every misalignment (see `im-confused-you-said.md`)
9. **Proposal** — anchored to documented gap, priced against outcome value (see [[ccc-proposal-draft]])
10. **Post-call** — update person file with notes that pass the CRM Challenge (see [[ccc-post-call-log]])

## Key principles (non-negotiable)

1. Sales are about them, not you.
2. Sell change, not product.
3. Discover before pitching, every time.
4. Make the buyer defend objections; never defend product.
5. Be a partner, not a servant — trade unreasonable demands.
6. Never sell to need; sell to problems and outcomes (the buyer often doesn't know what they actually need).
7. The close happens at the beginning, not the end.

## Failure-mode triggers (stop the call, loop back)

- Talking more than 50% during discovery → stop, ask another question
- Open-ended answer accepted → loop back, demand specificity
- Demo planned with > 6 features → kill features
- About to defend product/price → reframe with *"I'm confused. You said…"*
- Couldn't pass the CRM Challenge for these notes → discovery was too shallow
