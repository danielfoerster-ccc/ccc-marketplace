# The Nine Truthbombs of Selling — Quick Reference

*Reference card for the [[ccc-sales-process]] plugin. Source: [[Gap Selling — Keenan]] Ch. 2.*

---

## The principles, with operator implications

| # | Truthbomb | Operator Implication |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **No problem, no sale** | If you can't quantify a problem the buyer agrees they have, walk. |
| 2 | **In every sale there's a gap** | The gap (Future − Current) IS the value. Sell the gap, not the product. |
| 3 | **All sales are about change** | You are not selling product, you are selling change. |
| 4 | **Customers don't like change** | Even when they say they do — longevity bias is real, status-quo is the default. |
| 5 | **Sales are emotional** | Kanter's 10 change-resistance threats apply (see `kanter-10-threats.md`). |
| 6 | **Customers like change when it's worth the cost** | Show the math: outcome value > cost of switching, in their currency. |
| 7 | **Asking "Why?" gets to "Yes"** | Intrinsic motivation lives behind the stated goal. Surface it before pitching. |
| 8 | **Sales happen when future state > current state** | Anchor the buyer in their future state; make the future feel concrete. |
| 9 | **No one gives a shit about you** | Show-me economy. Don't open meetings with company history; lead with the buyer's problem. |

## How to use this card

- **In pre-call prep**: scan for which truthbombs are most at risk in this specific call. (E.g., a buyer who claims to want change but has resisted twice → Truthbomb 4 is active; lead with low-risk demonstration.)
- **In discovery**: if you catch yourself violating one (talking about your company, defending product, accepting an open-ended answer), stop the call internally and reset.
- **In troubleshooting**: when a deal stalls, diagnose which truthbomb you skipped. Most stalls trace to #1 (problem unquantified), #5 (emotional state ignored), or #7 (intrinsic motivation never surfaced).
