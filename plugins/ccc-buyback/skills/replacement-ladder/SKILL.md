# Replacement Ladder Skill

**Workflow: Audit Review → Identify Next Rung → Build Profile → Test-First Method → Output**

The goal isn't to grow a team. The goal is to buy back your time so you can produce more. Hire in sequence, test before committing, and sell the role once you've decided.

Session time: ~40 minutes.

---

## Background: The Replacement Ladder

You don't hire randomly. You climb a ladder — each rung unlocks the next. The sequence matters because each hire frees up time and attention that makes the following hire viable.

**The 5 Rungs:**

| Rung | Role | What they free up |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Personal / Admin Assistant** | Scheduling, email management, travel, logistics, admin tasks — the daily friction |
| 2 | **Marketing / Content Admin** | Content scheduling, social media management, basic marketing ops |
| 3 | **Junior Delivery** | Client work that doesn't require your senior judgment yet |
| 4 | **Senior Delivery / Specialist** | Complex client work, technical delivery, senior-level execution |
| 5 | **Leadership / Operations** | Management, team coordination, strategic oversight |

Most operators try to hire at rung 3 or 4 first because it feels like the most obvious bottleneck. But without rung 1, every rung above it is undermined — because you're still drowning in admin while trying to manage someone.

**The 10-80-10 Rule (applies to everything you delegate):**
- **10%** — You define the outcome (what does done look like?)
- **80%** — They execute (trust the process)
- **10%** — You review (feedback, not redoing)

If you're spending more than 20% of your time on any delegated task, you haven't truly delegated it.

## Phase 1 — Current Situation (3 questions, ~10 min)

**Q1 — Revenue check**
"What's current monthly revenue — roughly? This determines which rung is realistic to hire at now. (No judgment — just calibration.)"

Calibrate accordingly:
- Pre-revenue / under €1k/month → rung 1 is likely premature; focus on Claude Cowork for admin tasks
- €1k–€3k/month → rung 1 (PA/admin, part-time or freelance) is the right first hire
- €3k–€8k/month → rung 1 in place or being built; rung 2 may be relevant
- €8k+/month → rungs 1–2 should be filled or in progress; rung 3 becomes viable

**Q2 — What's stealing your time**
"If a Buyback Audit exists, I can pull the top delegation tasks. If not — what are the 5–10 things you're doing every week that you shouldn't be? What would you hand off first if someone good walked in tomorrow?"

*If audit exists: populate from the Delegate/Replace quadrant automatically.*

**Q3 — Pain point**
"What's the specific pain right now — is it that you can't scale delivery (too much client work to do yourself), you can't scale outreach (too much admin to do sales), or something else?"

---

## Phase 2 — Identify the Next Rung (~10 min)

Based on revenue, pain point, and delegation tasks, identify which rung to hire for next.

State the recommendation clearly:
> "Based on where you are, your next hire is **[Rung X: Role Name]**. Here's why: [1–2 sentence rationale]."

Then for that rung, define:
- What tasks they take off your plate (from the audit)
- What that frees you to do (Production Quadrant work)
- What success looks like in 90 days
- Estimated time to hire and time to productivity

---

## Phase 3 — Build the Role Profile (~10 min)

For the identified rung:

**Q4 — Outcomes, not activities**
"What are the 3–5 outcomes this person needs to achieve in their first 90 days? Not tasks — outcomes. What would make you say 'that was the right hire'?"

Use the Definition of Done framework for each outcome:
- **Facts**: what measurable results must be true?
- **Feelings**: how should the relevant parties feel?
- **Functionality**: what must it enable you (or clients) to do?

**Q5 — Ideal profile**
"What kind of person succeeds in this role? Think about: energy type (high initiative vs. follows clear instructions), background, key skills, working style preferences. And critically — what profile would *complement* your working style, not mirror it?"

**Q6 — What you don't want**
"What would a bad hire in this role look like? What behaviors or gaps would cause problems? Being specific here prevents hiring someone who looks good on paper but fails in practice."

---

## Phase 4 — Test-First Hiring Method (~5 min)

Don't commit to anyone before you've worked with them. The 6-step process:

**Step 1 — Be Clear**
Before posting anything: write out exactly what you're hiring for, using the outcomes from Phase 3. Vague job descriptions attract vague candidates.

**Step 2 — Cast a Wide Net**
Post to relevant job boards. Ask your network for referrals — team referrals specifically are the highest quality source. Consider approaching people at companies you admire whose style of work you respect.

**Step 3 — Video Screen First**
Ask all applicants to submit a 2–3 minute video answering 3–5 specific questions before any live interview. This single step eliminates 80% of unsuitable candidates and reveals communication ability, initiative, and genuine interest — none of which a CV shows.

Suggested screening questions:
- "Tell me what you know about [company/context] and why this role interests you."
- "Describe a specific situation where you [relevant skill/behavior]."
- "What would you do in your first 30 days to demonstrate value?"

**Step 4 — Profile Assessments**
For shortlisted candidates: use Kolbe-A (conative style), DISC (behavioral style), or similar. The goal isn't to filter for one profile — it's to understand how they'll work and whether they complement the team's existing strengths.

**Step 5 — Test-First (paid task)**
Before any offer: give the top 1–2 candidates a real, paid task from the actual job. This should be something that would normally take them 2–4 hours. Pay them for it. Evaluate based on quality, process, communication during the task, and how they handle ambiguity.

The principle: "I can't work with you until I work with you." No test, no hire.

**Step 6 — Sell the Future**
Once you've decided who you want: switch modes entirely. Stop interviewing — start recruiting. Align the role to their 3–5 year personal goals. Show them how working with you gets them there. The best candidates have options. Make them want this one.

## Rules

- Never recommend skipping rungs unless there's a compelling specific reason. The ladder sequence exists because each rung unlocks the next.
- If the operator is pre-revenue or under €1k/month, be honest: the first hire isn't a person — it's Claude Cowork + better processes. Frame that clearly and don't suggest premature hiring.
- The Test-First step is non-negotiable. It's the single highest-ROI step in the hiring process and the one operators skip most often. Explain why it matters.
- The "Sell the Future" step surprises operators but is critical for top candidates. Don't leave it out of the output.
- Never let the operator hire for skills alone. Hire for complement to the team's existing profile.
- Keep all output in English.
