## Key Insights to Encode in Every Plan

These are the things that make the difference between a plan that gets executed and one that sits in a folder:

**The One Thing** — At zero-client stage, direct network outreach is almost always the only channel that produces clients in weeks rather than months. Name this clearly, even if the user has other channels in mind.

**The Two-Brand Tension** — When personal brand leads (bigger LinkedIn, stronger network, existing content), use it. Many early-stage founders underuse their personal brand because they want the company to stand alone. The plan should resolve this tension explicitly.

**The Delivery Flywheel** — When the business builds systems for clients, every engagement is also product development. Client 3's automation becomes a plugin; Client 7's onboarding becomes a template. This reframing makes delivery feel like acceleration, not overhead.

**Honest Stretch Targets** — When a user names an ambitious timeline (e.g., "10 clients in 11 days"), acknowledge it directly. Give them the honest range (what's realistic vs. what's extraordinary), and explain what "good" looks like. Plans that only reflect best-case scenarios erode trust when reality hits.

**The 90-Day Hypothesis** — The plan is a learning instrument. One question, answered by day 90, unlocks everything downstream. Pricing, offer focus, retention model, plugin roadmap — all of it waits on real data.
