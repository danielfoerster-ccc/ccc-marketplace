# Stage 1: Frontier Prompting Reflex

## The Three Techniques

All reflex mastery begins with understanding what makes a question "frontier-shaped." Stage 1 teaches Daniel (or any operator) to recognize and apply three core techniques from Michael Simmons' field-processing framework.

### Technique 1: Pattern Bridge

**Core idea:** AI's native strength is to see unexpected parallels across distant domains. Instead of asking "how do I solve X?" ask "what patterns from [unrelated domains] all solving similar structural problems illuminate this?"

**What activates:** Cross-domain field synthesis. The question activates simultaneity across multiple constraint spaces.

**Signal that Pattern Bridge is needed:**
- The question is too vague or has too many degrees of freedom
- The operator is stuck in linear thinking ("step 1, step 2, step 3")
- The problem has a structural pattern that exists in other domains too

**Example transformation:**
- Original: "How can I make my audience bigger?"
- Pattern Bridge: "What patterns from urban planning, mycology (fungal networks), jazz improvisation, and organizational scaling all use simultaneously to grow reach while maintaining coherence? What's the structural parallel?"

### Technique 2: Void Space

**Core idea:** Breakthrough innovations live at phase transitions — in the boundaries between established approaches. Instead of "what's best practice?" ask "what exists in the space between established frameworks?"

**What activates:** Boundary exploration. The question dissolves artificial constraints and accesses possibility spaces where novel models live.

**Signal that Void Space is needed:**
- The question seeks optimization within known territory (better pricing model, faster hiring, etc.)
- The operator is missing the adjacent possibility space where genuinely new approaches live
- The question has implicit boundaries that could be dissolved

**Example transformation:**
- Original: "What's the best pricing model for a SaaS company?"
- Void Space: "What pricing models would emerge if we dissolved the boundary between 'access to software' and 'direct partnership'? What exists in the void between subscription and equity?"

### Technique 3: Quantum Leap

**Core idea:** The future is not a single path — it's a superposition of possibilities. Instead of "what will happen?" ask "what becomes visible when we access all plausible futures simultaneously?"

**What activates:** Multi-trajectory simultaneity. The question moves from sequential forecasting to simultaneous possibility mapping.

**Signal that Quantum Leap is needed:**
- The question is about strategic futures or "what should we do?"
- Linear forecasting (step-by-step roadmapping) is missing breakthrough paths
- The operator needs to see what connects across multiple possible futures

**Example transformation:**
- Original: "What should our company do in the next 3 years?"
- Quantum Leap: "If we could access all plausible 3-year futures simultaneously — not as alternatives but as a superposition — what unexpected paths only become visible when we hold them all at once?"

## Reflex Mastery Definition

**Reflex mastery = the operator asks a frontier-shaped question without being prompted to reshape it.**

This is not about knowledge. It's about intuition. After 7 days of daily practice, the operator's brain internalises the pattern. They stop asking "what's the answer?" and start asking "what field do I need to activate?"

The skill detects reflex mastery by asking: "Was your original question already frontier-shaped?" If YES, that's a reflex day. Seven YES days in a row = Stage 1 graduation.

## Failure Modes & Recovery

### Failure Mode 1: Missed Days (3+ days)

**What happens:** The operator skips practice for 3+ days (vacation, busy period, etc.).

**Skill response:** Gentle re-engagement.
```
You took a few days off — that's normal. 
Let's pick up where we left off.
```

Reset `consecutive_reflex_days` to 0, but keep the stage as "in progress" — never "abandoned."

### Failure Mode 2: No AI Question Needed

**What happens:** The operator's TOP 3 task doesn't need an AI question (e.g., "make a phone call", "send an email").

**Skill response:** That's okay.
```
No AI questions needed today? That's fine. 
That actually means you've learned discrimination — knowing when NOT to ask is part of the reflex.
```

Count it as a practice day. (The operator has learned the boundary of when frontier prompting applies.)

### Failure Mode 3: Question Doesn't Transform Well

**What happens:** One of the three techniques doesn't produce a useful frontier version for a particular question.

**Skill response:** The operator picks the version that works best, or stays with the original if none feel right.

This is not a failure — it's learning. Update the Rules section to note which question types resist certain techniques.
