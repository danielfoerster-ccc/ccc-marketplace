# Branches — Question banks per branch

Read this file when about to enter Phase 2 questioning, after the branch decision is made.

---

## Strategic / Decision-Making Branch

**Front-weighted dimensions:** Decision History · Frameworks · Constraints
**Allocate ~50% of questioning time to these three; ~50% across the other four.**

### Dimension 1 — Values (lighter touch in this branch — 1-2 questions)

- "When you're choosing between two attractive options in this domain, what's the deeper criterion that breaks the tie?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "If I gave you a choice that violated that value but doubled the upside, would you really say no? When have you compromised on it?"

### Dimension 2 — Thinking Style (1-2 questions)

- "When you face a decision in this domain, what's your default mode — write it out, talk it through, sleep on it, decide fast?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "Walk me through the last decision in this domain — actual mode used. Did it match your default, or did you do something different?"

### Dimension 3 — Blindspots (lighter — 1 question, but apply heavy pushback)

- "Where in this domain have advisors / partners / coaches consistently pushed back on your reasoning?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "You said your blindspot is X. Is that what they actually said, or your sanitised version of it? Quote the exact pushback if you can."

### Dimension 4 — Frameworks (HEAVY — 3-4 questions)

- "Which mental models do you reach for first in this domain?"
- "Which framework do you trust most when two perspectives conflict?"
- "Which framework have you decided NOT to use here, and why? (this surfaces priors)"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "You named [framework]. When did it fail you in this domain? What did you replace it with, even temporarily?"

### Dimension 5 — Decision History (HEAVIEST — 4-5 questions, 30%+ of total time)

The pattern of past decisions reveals priors better than any self-report. Spend time here.

- "What's the most consequential decision you've made in this domain? Walk me through it — the moment, what you weighed, what you decided, what happened."
- "What are the last 3-5 consequential decisions? Pattern across them?"
- "What's a decision you avoided making? What was the cost of avoidance?"
- "What's a decision you wish you'd made differently? What would v2 look like?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "Your stated framework is X, but the decisions you described actually applied Y. Which is the real one?"

### Dimension 6 — Constraints (HEAVY — 3 questions)

- "What can't you spend in this domain? Time, money, attention, risk, political capital?"
- "Which constraints are real vs. inherited? What would change if a constraint dissolved?"
- "What's the non-negotiable obligation that pre-shapes every choice?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "You said the constraint is time. If I gave you 10× the time tomorrow, what would actually change? If nothing — time isn't the real constraint."

### Dimension 7 — Goals (1-2 questions)

- "What does success in this domain look like 6-12 months from now? Specific enough that you'd know whether you got there."
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "If you hit that goal but everything else in your life stayed the same, would you actually feel successful? What's the goal behind the goal?"

## Learning / Coaching / Creative Branch

**Front-weighted dimensions:** Values · Thinking Style · Blindspots
**Allocate ~50% of questioning time to these three; ~50% across the other four.**

### Dimension 1 — Values (HEAVY — 3-4 questions)

- "In this domain, what's non-negotiable? What would you walk away from?"
- "What does 'good work' / 'good learning' / 'good output' look like — by your own taste, not by external praise?"
- "What does *bad* work in this domain feel like? Where does the disgust kick in?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "You said you value [X]. When have you violated it? What did that situation reveal about how you actually rank values vs. how you say you rank them?"

### Dimension 2 — Thinking Style (HEAVY — 3-4 questions)

- "How do you actually process information here — fast/intuitive, slow/deliberate, written/verbal, alone/with input?"
- "When do you trust your gut in this domain vs. defer to research / analysis / advisors?"
- "What's your relationship to discomfort in this domain? (over-push, withdraw, oscillate)"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "You described yourself as X-style. Walk me through the last hour you spent on this domain. Did the behaviour match the description, or were you actually doing something else?"

### Dimension 3 — Blindspots (HEAVIEST — 4-5 questions, 30%+ of time)

This is where the operator most needs help — they can't self-report what they don't see. Push hardest here.

- "Where in this domain do you suspect you have a blindspot, even if you can't name it precisely?"
- "Who's pushed back on your thinking here, and what did they push on?"
- "What's a pattern in your work others have named that surprised you?"
- "What do you avoid in this domain — and is the avoidance the blindspot itself?"
- *Disconfirming pushback (apply twice):* "You named blindspot X. That sounds like a 'safe' blindspot — easy to admit. What's an *unsafe* one — something you'd be embarrassed for someone to know?"

### Dimension 4 — Frameworks (medium — 2-3 questions)

- "Which mental models or thinkers shape how you approach this domain?"
- "Which framework have you outgrown or rejected, and why?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "You named [Simmons / Stutz / Tegmark / etc.]. Where do you disagree with them, even slightly?"

### Dimension 5 — Decision History (medium — 2 questions)

- "What's a meaningful turning point in this domain — when something clicked or broke?"
- "What's a pattern across your last 3-5 sessions / pieces / efforts here?"

### Dimension 6 — Constraints (medium — 2 questions)

- "What constraints shape your work in this domain — time, energy, identity, role, life-stage?"
- "Which constraint, if removed, would actually change what you produce?"

### Dimension 7 — Goals (medium — 2 questions)

- "What does mastery / progress / completion look like in this domain over 6-12 months?"
- *Disconfirming pushback:* "Is that the *goal* or the *socially-acceptable goal*? What's the version you'd be embarrassed to say out loud?"

## Notes for the questioner

- **Never run all questions in a single dump.** One question (or tight 2-3-question cluster on the same dimension) per turn. Wait for the answer. Apply pushback. Then move on.
- **The pushback IS the value.** Without Disconfirming Evidence Questioning, the profile becomes a sanitised self-description — useless for system-prompt context.
- **Watch for fatigue.** 25-40 minutes is the right range for a full pass. If the operator gets tired before all 7 dimensions are covered, it's better to mark under-developed dimensions and ship v0.1 than to push for thin answers — those can be filled in at the first refresh.
- **Watch for genuine "I don't know" vs. evasion.** The first is a real signal (often points at where the operator hasn't lived enough yet); the second is what Disconfirming Evidence is for.
- **Cite operator-named vault nodes as wikilinks.** If they say "Stutz" or "Three Brains" — note `[[Phil Stutz]]` / `[[Three Brains]]` for inclusion in the final Profile Card.
